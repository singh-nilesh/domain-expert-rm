# Skill 02: Web Research & Information Gathering

## Purpose
Gather expert domain knowledge, real case studies, industry benchmarks, and best practices for this scenario.

## When to Use
- After creating research plan using `skills/01_planning.md`
- This is STEP 2 of the pipeline

## Goal

**Find:** Expert RM perspectives, mental models, real-world case studies with outcomes, and industry best practices

**NOT:** Technical documentation, SQL queries, system screenshots, or generic process steps

## Search Strategy

### Source Priorities

**Tier 1 (Best) - Consulting & Industry:**
- McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, PwC, Accenture banking reports
- American Banker, The Banker, Euromoney articles
- RMA, ABA, BBA white papers
- Named banking experts and thought leaders
- Federal Reserve, OCC banking guidance

**Tier 2 (Good) - Practitioner Content:**
- Banking technology vendors (with practitioner insights)
- Case studies from major banks
- Academic banking journals (practitioner-focused)
- Banking association research

**Avoid:**
- Generic business advice
- Unattributed or AI-generated content
- Technical documentation or SQL guides
- Anonymous blogs or unverified sources

### Search Query Format

Use banking terminology and expert phrasing:

✅ "How commercial banking experts identify cross-sell opportunities"
✅ "McKinsey share-of-wallet analysis corporate banking"
✅ "Expert frameworks for evaluating transaction banking penetration"
✅ "Case studies: global cash management implementation outcomes"
✅ "Banking industry benchmarks: client relationship profitability"

❌ "What is cross-sell?" (too generic)
❌ "SQL queries for customer data" (wrong focus)
❌ "How to use CRM software" (technical, not strategic)

## Information Extraction

For each source, capture:

**Expert Thinking:**
- How do experts approach this situation?
- What mental models or frameworks do they use?
- What decision criteria guide them?
- What do they consider before acting?

**Specific Numbers & Benchmarks:**
- What are industry standards or thresholds?
- What do successful RMs achieve?
- What are typical conversion rates, percentages, or metrics?

**Real Case Studies:**
- Company names and situations
- Actions taken
- Measured outcomes
- Key lessons or insights

**Best Practices Patterns:**
- What do top performers do differently?
- What pitfalls should be avoided?
- What tools or approaches do leaders use?

**Risk & Compliance:**
- What risks exist in this scenario?
- What regulatory or compliance considerations?
- How do experts manage these?

## Citation Standards

For each piece of information, capture:
- **Source:** Publication/firm name
- **Author (if named):** Expert or firm name
- **Date:** Publication date
- **URL:** Link to article/report
- **Quote:** Specific relevant text if notable

Example:
> "Share-of-wallet for mature corporate banking relationships ranges from 25-35%, but industry leaders consistently achieve 40%+ penetration through proactive cross-sell" — McKinsey, "Corporate Banking Excellence," 2023, [URL]

### Phase 3: Cross-Validate Findings

**Never rely on single sources for critical information.**

**For Numerical Data:**
- Find 2+ sources with similar figures
- If sources conflict, note the range
- Prefer recent over older data
- Prefer regulatory/official sources

**For Methodologies:**
- Look for consensus across multiple practitioners
- Check if consulting firms describe similar approaches
- Validate against regulatory guidance
- Test if methodology makes logical sense

**For Best Practices:**
- Confirm across multiple banking publications
- Verify alignment with regulatory expectations
- Ensure practices are current (not outdated)

**When Sources Conflict:**
1. Document both perspectives
2. Assess credibility of each source
3. Look for tie-breaker source
4. Present both with context in final document

## Search Strategy by Iteration

### Iteration 1: Exploratory Foundation (5-7 searches)
**Goal:** Understand scenario, find core methodologies, identify 1-2 examples

**Approach:** Start broad, then narrow; explore options; foundational content

**Success Criteria:**
- Found 3-5 credible sources
- Basic understanding of RM approach
- At least 1 concrete example
- Understand data requirements at high level

### Iteration 2: Targeted Gap-Filling (3-5 searches, if needed)
**Goal:** Address specific gaps; add more examples; validate calculations

**Approach:** Highly targeted queries; go deep; quality over quantity

**Success Criteria:**
- Filled 70%+ of identified gaps
- Found additional concrete example
- Validated key calculations
- Added specialized depth

### Iteration 3: Validation & Polish (2-3 searches, if needed)
**Goal:** Validate questionable info; find final missing pieces; confirm numbers

**Approach:** Precision searches; authoritative sources only; confirmation

**Success Criteria:**
- All key facts confirmed by 2+ sources
- No critical gaps remaining
- All benchmarks current (last 2-3 years)
- Expert perspective included

## Search Execution Framework

### Phase 1: Prepare for Search

**Review Requirements:**
1. What specific information am I looking for?
2. What would constitute a high-quality source for this?
3. What keywords will industry experts use?
4. What evidence will I need to cite?

**Set Search Expectations:**
- Iteration 1: Broad exploratory searches for foundational content
- Iteration 2: Targeted searches for specific gaps
- Iteration 3: Validation searches to confirm details

### Phase 2: Execute Searches

For each search query in your plan:

#### Step 1: Formulate Precise Query
**Use Banking-Specific Terminology:**
- ✅ "commercial banking relationship manager cross-sell methodology"
- ❌ "how to sell more to customers"

**Include Context Qualifiers:**
- Industry: "corporate banking", "commercial banking", "wholesale banking"
- Role: "relationship manager", "RM", "coverage banker"
- Function: "credit analysis", "portfolio management", "client service"
- Product: "transaction banking", "trade finance", "syndicated loans"

**Search Query Construction:**
```
[Core Topic] + [Banking Context] + [Content Type]

Examples:
- "share of wallet calculation" + "corporate banking" + "methodology"
- "cross-sell framework" + "relationship manager" + "best practices"
- "client profitability analysis" + "commercial banking" + "case study"
```

#### Step 2: Execute and Evaluate Results
For each search result, quickly assess:

**Source Quality Indicators:**
- ✅ Industry publications (American Banker, The Banker, Euromoney)
- ✅ Consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, PwC, Accenture)
- ✅ Banking associations (ABA, RMA, BBA)
- ✅ Regulatory bodies (OCC, Federal Reserve, Basel Committee)
- ✅ Banking technology vendors with practitioner content
- ✅ Academic banking journals with practitioner research
- ⚠️ Generic business sites (use cautiously, validate elsewhere)
- ⚠️ Individual blogs (use only if clear banking expertise)
- ❌ Obvious AI-generated content farms
- ❌ Unattributed or anonymous sources

**Content Quality Indicators:**
- ✅ Specific numbers, benchmarks, or formulas
- ✅ Named case studies with real outcomes
- ✅ Detailed step-by-step processes
- ✅ References to specific banking systems or tools
- ✅ Citations to other credible sources
- ⚠️ Generic advice without specifics
- ❌ Obvious marketing content without substance
- ❌ Contradicts known banking practices

#### Step 3: Deep Read Valuable Sources
For sources that pass quality assessment:

**Extract Systematically:**

1. **Facts and Data:**
   - Specific numbers (percentages, dollar amounts, ratios)
   - Benchmarks and thresholds
   - Statistical findings
   - Industry averages

2. **Methodologies and Frameworks:**
   - Step-by-step processes
   - Analytical frameworks
   - Decision trees or models
   - Calculation formulas

3. **Best Practices:**
   - What top performers do
   - Common approaches
   - Recommended actions
   - Success patterns

4. **Real Examples:**
   - Company names (if disclosed)
   - Situation descriptions
   - Actions taken
   - Outcomes achieved
   - Lessons learned

5. **Tools and Systems:**
   - Software platforms mentioned
   - Analytical tools used
   - Data sources referenced
   - Technology enablers

6. **Risks and Compliance:**
   - Regulatory requirements
   - Compliance considerations
   - Risk factors
   - Mitigation approaches

**Citation Capture:**
For EVERY piece of information extracted, record:
- Source title
- Author/organization
- Publication date
- URL
- Specific page/section (if applicable)

Format:
```
[Fact/Finding] 
Source: [Title], [Author/Org], [Date], [URL]
```

### Phase 3: Cross-Validate Findings

**Validation Strategy:**
Never rely on a single source for critical information. Seek confirmation:

**For Numerical Data:**
- Find at least 2 sources with similar figures
- If sources conflict, note the range and explain variance
- Prefer more recent data over older data
- Prefer regulatory/official sources over commercial estimates

**For Methodologies:**
- Look for consensus across multiple practitioners
- Check if consulting firms describe similar approaches
- Validate against regulatory guidance when applicable
- Test if methodology makes logical sense

**For Best Practices:**
- Confirm across multiple banking publications
- Check if recommended by credible consulting firms
- Verify alignment with regulatory expectations
- Ensure practices are current (not outdated)

**When Sources Conflict:**
1. Document both perspectives
2. Assess credibility of each source
3. Look for tie-breaker source
4. Present both with context in final document
5. Recommend validation approach for RM

## Information Quality Assessment

For each piece of information, assess:

### Credibility Score (1-10)
**10 - Highest:** Regulatory guidance, official bank publications, peer-reviewed research
**8-9:** Top consulting firms, industry associations, leading banking publications
**6-7:** Banking technology vendors, industry experts with credentials
**4-5:** General business publications covering banking topics
**1-3:** Blogs, anonymous sources, unverified claims

**Use only sources scoring 6+ for core content**
**Use only sources scoring 8+ for critical facts**

### Recency Score
- Published in last 12 months: Excellent
- Published in last 2-3 years: Good (validate if still current)
- Published 3-5 years ago: Acceptable (validate best practices haven't changed)
- Published 5+ years ago: Use cautiously (banking tech and practices evolve)

### Specificity Score
- Contains specific numbers, formulas, named examples: Excellent
- Contains detailed processes with some specifics: Good
- Contains general guidance with some detail: Acceptable
- Contains only generic advice: Insufficient

## Common Search Pitfalls

### Avoid:
❌ **Stopping at first result:** First result isn't always best
❌ **Generic business content:** "customer satisfaction tips" instead of banking-specific
❌ **Undated content:** Can't verify if current
❌ **Marketing disguised as content:** White papers that are really product pitches
❌ **Over-reliance on one source:** Need multiple confirmations
❌ **Ignoring recency:** Banking practices evolve; old content may be obsolete
❌ **Skipping source evaluation:** Not all web content is credible
❌ **Copying without understanding:** Must comprehend to synthesize well

### Do:
✅ **Dig into search results pages:** Good content often on page 2-3
✅ **Use specific banking jargon:** Gets you past generic content
✅ **Check multiple source types:** Publications, firms, regulators, vendors
✅ **Validate numbers:** Cross-check statistics and benchmarks
✅ **Note publication dates:** Ensure currency
✅ **Read full articles:** Summaries miss critical details
✅ **Follow references:** Good articles cite other good sources
✅ **Save URLs immediately:** Don't lose track of sources
