# Research Plan: Scenario 022 - Approaching Credit Limit Warning

## Scenario Summary
In the high-growth corporate banking segment, liquidity management is a dynamic game. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) do not wait for a "Limit Breached" alert; instead, they utilize proactive monitoring of facility utilization to anticipate a client's needs. This scenario focuses on the 85% utilization threshold—the "Danger Zone" where an RM must decide whether to support the client's growth with a limit increase or pivot to risk mitigation. By synthesizing transaction velocity, financial KPIs, and internal risk ratings, the RM delivers a proactive solution (e.g., an interim overline or a formal increase) that ensures the client's operations are never disrupted by a funding block.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for managing clients approaching their credit limits.
Secondary: Identify specific "Danger Zone" thresholds, limit increase approval protocols, and the best practices for pivoting to non-credit solutions when capacity is limited.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "85% Rule" Framework:** Why is 85% considered the standard "Trigger Point" for a credit limit warning? What are the lead-lag indicators of a breach?
2. **Predictive Utilization Modeling:** How do expert RMs use transaction velocity (from the Detailed Ledger) to predict the *exact date* of a limit breach?
3. **The "Expansion vs. Risk" Decision Tree:** What criteria (ROE, Credit Rating, Industry Outlook) determine if an RM should pitch a limit increase or a limit freeze?
4. **Interim Overline Mechanics:** What are the standard terms and approval paths for "Temporary Overlines" to bridge a 90-day liquidity spike?
5. **Pivoting to Asset-Based Solutions:** How do RMs use Supply Chain Finance or Receivables Factoring as "Credit-Light" alternatives when the balance sheet limit is capped?
6. **Data Signals for Success:** What specific fields in the core schema (Monthly Aggregate, KPI History) reveal if a drawdown is for "Healthy Growth" vs. "Liquidity Stress"?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for proactive limit management (e.g., Time-to-Increase, Breach Avoidance Rate, Limit Utilization Efficiency)?
