# Research Plan: Scenario 6 - FX and Derivatives Product Activation

## Scenario Summary
The RM identifies clients with significant international trade (imports/exports) or variable-rate debt who are not currently using the bank’s FX or interest rate hedging products. By analyzing transaction data and financial statements, the RM quantifies the "Unhedged Exposure" and pitches derivatives (Forwards, Options, Swaps) as a way to protect profit margins and ensure cash flow predictability. The goal is to activate the FX/Derivatives product line and increase high-margin fee income.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Understand the diagnostic markers expert RMs use to identify unhedged currency and interest rate risk and the "Consultative Selling" approach used for derivatives.
Secondary: Identify industry benchmarks for "FX Wallet Share," typical "Hedge Ratios" by industry, and the regulatory requirements (e.g., Dodd-Frank/EMIR) for corporate derivative sales.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do expert RMs frame derivatives for corporate clients? (Risk Mitigation vs. Speculation).
2. **Decision Logic:** What specific triggers (e.g., cross-border payments, upcoming floating-rate reset) indicate a need for hedging? How do experts prioritize FX vs. IR derivatives?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What is the average "FX-to-Trade-Volume" ratio for mid-market vs. large corporate clients? What are standard "Hedge Ratios" for manufacturing firms?
4. **Calculations:** How do RMs calculate the "Value at Risk" (VaR) or "Earnings at Risk" (EaR) for an unhedged corporate client?
5. **Best Practices:** How do top RMs overcome the "Derivatives are Risky" stigma with CFOs? What are the most effective "Simple" products to lead with?
6. **Risk Management:** How do banks manage the "Counterparty Credit Risk" of the derivatives themselves? What is "Credit Support Annex" (CSA) and when is it needed?
7. **Case Studies:** Examples of banks successfully activating FX/Derivatives product lines for previously "Vanilla" clients.
