# Research Plan: Scenario 3 - Deposit Growth Campaign Targeting

## Scenario Summary
The RM identifies clients with significant liquidity (cash on hand) but low deposit balances at the bank. By analyzing the "Deposit Wallet," interest rate sensitivity, and liquidity needs, the RM develops a targeted campaign to attract operational deposits (operating accounts) and non-operational deposits (excess cash/investments). The goal is to improve the bank's Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR) and reduce the cost of funds.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Understand how expert RMs distinguish between "sticky" operational deposits and "volatile" non-operational deposits and the strategies used to win both.
Secondary: Identify industry benchmarks for "Deposit Share of Wallet," typical pricing strategies for corporate deposits (e.g., Earning Credit Rate - ECR), and the impact of corporate deposits on bank LCR.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do expert RMs categorize deposits? What makes a deposit "Operational" vs. "Non-Operational" in the eyes of a treasurer?
2. **Decision Logic:** When should an RM lead with "Yield" (interest rate) versus "Service/Integration" (Treasury Management)?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What are the standard "Deposit-to-Loan" ratios for corporate clients across different industries?
4. **Calculations:** How do RMs calculate the **Earnings Credit Rate (ECR)** and the **Net Value of Deposits** to the bank?
5. **Best Practices:** What are the most effective strategies for winning "Operating Accounts" (the "holy grail" of deposits)? 
6. **Risk Management:** How do "Concentrated Deposits" affect the bank's risk profile? What are the lessons from recent banking stresses (e.g., SVB) regarding corporate deposit stability?
7. **Case Studies:** Examples of banks successfully executing deposit growth campaigns in a high-interest-rate or volatile environment.
