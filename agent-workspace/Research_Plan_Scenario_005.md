# Research Plan: Scenario 5 - Credit Renewal Proactive Engagement

## Scenario Summary
The RM identifies a credit facility expiring in the next 6-12 months. Instead of waiting for a reactive renewal, the RM proactively engages the client to discuss their future funding needs, manage interest rate risk (e.g., locking in rates), and optimize the facility structure. The goal is to prevent "Renewal Shock," block competitors, and use the renewal as a "wedge" to deepen the relationship.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Document the "Proactive Renewal Playbook" used by top-tier RMs, including the timeline for engagement and the key diagnostic questions asked.
Secondary: Identify industry benchmarks for "Renewal Retention Rates," typical "Step-up" or "Step-down" pricing strategies in the current rate environment, and the impact of early renewals on bank capital.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do expert RMs view the "Renewal Window"? (Risk vs. Opportunity).
2. **Decision Logic:** When should an RM recommend an "Early Extension" versus a "Full Refinance"? What are the triggers (e.g., rate volatility, covenant headroom)?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What is the industry-standard "Early Engagement" window (6, 9, or 12 months)? What are current "Renewal Retention" targets for corporate banks?
4. **Calculations:** How do RMs calculate the **Break-even Rate** for an early renewal versus waiting for maturity?
5. **Best Practices:** How do experts use the renewal to "right-size" covenants or "up-sell" ancillary products?
6. **Risk Management:** How do proactive renewals mitigate **Refinance Risk** and **Concentration Risk**?
7. **Case Studies:** Examples of banks using proactive renewals to defend against aggressive competition or market volatility.
