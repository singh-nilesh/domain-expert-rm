# Scenario 001: High-Wallet Low-Capture Cross-Sell Targeting

## Executive Summary
Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) view a "High-Wallet Low-Capture" client not merely as a sales target, but as a strategic "under-penetrated asset" where the bank's current credit commitment is disproportionate to its fee-based revenue. In this scenario, the RM’s primary objective is to close the "Wallet Gap" by identifying specific product buckets (Trade, FX, Cash Management) where the client is currently using competitors. The core intent is to transform a "Lending-only" or "Lending-heavy" relationship into a "Primary Bank" relationship, which industry benchmarks show can increase relationship ROE by 200-300 basis points. Success is measured by the delta in **Share of Wallet (SoW)** and the conversion of the "Addressable Wallet" into realized revenue.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** This scenario typically surfaces during the Annual Account Planning cycle or when a "Wallet Intelligence" tool flags a discrepancy between a client's business scale (Firmographics) and the bank's internal revenue run-rate.
- **Stakeholders:** RM (Lead), Product Specialists (Trade, FX, Treasury), Credit Risk (for limit verification), and the Client’s CFO/Treasurer.
- **Challenge:** The primary challenge is "Competitive Entrenchment." If a client has a $50M wallet but only gives 15% to your bank, the remaining 85% is held by rivals who likely have deep operational integrations (e.g., ERP connectivity for cash management).

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply a "Consultative Value-Add" mental model rather than a "Product-Push" mindset.

### Initial Assessment: The "Right to Win"
Before pitching, top performers ask: 
1. **Is the wallet "Addressable"?** (Does the client have operations in our footprint? Does our risk appetite match their industry?)
2. **What is the "Anchor" product?** (If we already have the lending, we have the "right to win" the ancillary business.)
3. **What is the "Competitive Barrier"?** (Why haven't we captured this yet? Is it pricing, technology, or service?)

### Mental Models: The Wallet Opportunity Matrix
Experts use this 2x2 framework to prioritize:
- **High Potential / Low SOW (Growth Targets):** The primary focus of this scenario.
- **High Potential / High SOW (Core Clients):** Defensive play to protect existing revenue.
- **Low Potential / High SOW (Niche Clients):** Efficiency play (digital-first).
- **Low Potential / Low SOW (Transactional):** Low priority.

### Decision Trees: Product Prioritization
- **If the client has cross-border flows** → Prioritize FX and Trade Finance.
- **If the client has high transaction volumes but low balances** → Prioritize Cash Management (Integrated Receivables).
- **If the client is expanding CapEx** → Prioritize Equipment Finance or IB Advisory.

## Expert Actions & Execution
Top RMs follow a 5-step execution pipeline:

### 1. Discovery & Wallet Sizing
- **Data Review:** Analyze internal transaction data to find "leakage" (e.g., identifying competitor names in outgoing wire transfers).
- **External Benchmarking:** Use tools like **Coalition Greenwich** or **Dun & Bradstreet** to estimate the client’s total spend based on revenue and industry (e.g., manufacturing firms spend ~0.05% of revenue on treasury services).

### 2. Gap Analysis & Financial Modeling
- **Calculate SoW Gap:** 
  - Formula: `Incremental Revenue Potential = Total Wallet Size x (Target Share - Current Share)`
  - Example: A $50M wallet at 15% SOW has a $7.5M current capture. To reach a 40% "Lead Bank" status, the RM targets an additional $12.5M.

### 3. Verification of Credit & Risk Capacity
- **Credit Check:** Confirm existing funded/unfunded limits. Expert RMs know that proposing more business often requires "Headroom."
- **Risk Assessment:** Verify internal ratings and industry guardrails. Proposing FX hedging to a client with a deteriorating credit rating will be blocked by Credit Risk.

### 4. Consultative Pitching (The "Value-Add")
- **Margin Protection (FX/Trade):** Frame FX as protecting the margin of the trade finance we already provide.
- **Operational Efficiency (Cash):** Pitch integrated ERP solutions that reduce the client's manual reconciliation time.

### 5. Internal Coordination
- **Squad Formation:** Bring in Trade/Treasury specialists. RMs lead the relationship, but specialists "close" the product.

## Real-World Examples & Case Studies

### Case Study 1: JPMorgan Chase - AI-Driven "Next Best Action"
- **Situation:** JPM implemented AI workbenches to analyze client data and suggest cross-sell opportunities to RMs.
- **Outcome:** The bank achieved a **35% rise in cross-sell revenue** for targeted products. The system identified high-wallet clients with low product "attachment" scores, allowing RMs to focus on the 20% of the portfolio representing 80% of the growth potential.

### Case Study 2: DBS Bank - The "ADA" Nudge Engine
- **Situation:** DBS used its "ADA" platform to send hyper-personalized context-aware prompts to RMs about client international expansions.
- **Outcome:** In 2025, these "nudges" delivered **SGD 150 million in incremental revenue**. Conversion rates for complex trade finance products improved by **50%** because the pitch was timed to a specific "trigger event" (a large foreign currency payment).

### Case Study 3: McKinsey Pilot - Regional Bank Success
- **Situation:** A US regional bank used McKinsey's AA (Advanced Analytics) workbench to identify "wallet leakage."
- **Outcome:** RMs using the tool saw **9% portfolio growth** vs. **5%** for the control group. The bank is on track for a **20% revenue increase over 3 years** by focusing on the "Wallet Gap" in the middle-market segment.

## Industry Best Practices
- **The "3-Product Rule":** Industry data (Citigroup) shows that clients using 3+ services have a **retention rate 2x higher** than single-product clients.
- **Primary Bank Status Targets:** Aim for **30-40% SoW** for lead relationships. Most banks overestimate their share by **30-50%**; experts use "Wallet Intelligence" to get real.
- **Solution Bundling:** Bundle FX spread tightening with Trade Finance volume. This "Relationship Pricing" makes it harder for competitors to pick off individual product lines.
- **Avoid "Product Pushing":** Top performers spend 70% of the meeting asking discovery questions about the client's *business* and 30% on *products*.

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **CRM/Relationship Workbenches:** (e.g., nCino, Salesforce Financial Services Cloud) with AI "Next Best Offer" widgets.
- **Wallet Intelligence Platforms:** (e.g., Coalition Greenwich, Alkami, Personetics) for external benchmarking.
- **ERP Connectors:** (e.g., Fispan) to integrate banking services directly into the client's software (NetSuite, SAP).
- **RAROC/ROE Calculators:** To model the profitability of the proposed cross-sell.

## Client Impact & Value Proposition
- **Quantified Benefit:** "By consolidating your FX hedging with your trade finance provider, you reduce settlement risk by **100%** and lower your administrative overhead by **~15%**."
- **ROI:** Show how automated receivables can reduce "Days Sales Outstanding" (DSO) by 3-5 days, unlocking millions in working capital.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **Concentration Risk:** Avoid over-leveraging a single high-wallet client. If the bank captures 80% of a client's business, a client default is catastrophic.
- **Compliance:** Ensure all cross-selling follows "Anti-Tying" regulations (e.g., Section 106 of the Bank Holding Company Act in the US). You cannot *force* a client to use FX as a condition of a loan.
- **Revenue at Risk (RaR):** Calculate RaR to prioritize defense. If a high-wallet client is "High RaR," the RM focuses on service recovery before cross-selling.
