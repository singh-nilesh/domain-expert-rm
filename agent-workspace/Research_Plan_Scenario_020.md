# Research Plan: Scenario 020 - Referral-Based Cross-Sell from Existing Clients

## Scenario Summary
In the high-trust world of corporate banking, a "Warm Referral" from a satisfied CFO or Treasurer is 10x more valuable than a cold outreach. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) view their existing portfolio not just as a revenue source, but as a **Referral Ecosystem.** This scenario focuses on identifying "Client Ambassadors"—highly satisfied, digitally entrenched clients with strong industry networks—and systematically soliciting introductions to their suppliers, customers, or professional peers. By leveraging High NPS (Net Promoter Score) and demonstrating the "Network Alpha" (e.g., "We already handle your main supplier, so we can automate your trade finance"), the RM secures high-probability leads with shortened sales cycles and pre-validated credit credibility.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for generating and converting referral-based leads.
Secondary: Identify specific "Referral Triggers," B2B referral incentive models, and the psychology of the "Peer-to-Peer" recommendation in corporate finance.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "Ambassador" Profile:** What are the key indicators that a client is likely to provide a referral (e.g., NPS > 8, usage of 5+ products, frequent event attendance)?
2. **Identifying Referral Channels:** How do expert RMs map a client's business network (Suppliers, Buyers, Board connections) to identify target prospects?
3. **The "Referral Ask" Script:** How does an RM successfully ask for a referral without sounding transactional or desperate?
4. **Referral Incentives in B2B:** What are the industry-standard (and compliant) ways to reward a referring client (e.g., "Relationship Credits," exclusive event access, "Ambassador Tiers")?
5. **The "Network Alpha" Pitch:** How do RMs use the existing relationship to "sell" the prospect (e.g., "Because we bank your parent company, your onboarding will take 10 days instead of 90")?
6. **Data Signals for Referrals:** What signals in the CRM (positive call reports, high satisfaction scores) indicate the "Perfect Moment" to ask for a referral?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for a "Referral Program" (e.g., Referral Yield, Conversion Rate of Warm Leads, Time-to-Revenue for Referrals)?
