# Corporate Banking RM AI Agent - Scenario Knowledge Base

## Purpose
This document contains 120+ foundational scenarios that define how the AI agent should think and act like a Corporate Banking Relationship Manager. Each scenario represents a real-world situation where the agent must combine data from multiple tables, apply banking logic, and deliver proactive, actionable insights.

## Category 1: Cross-Sell & Revenue Growth Opportunities

### Scenario 1: High-Wallet Low-Capture Cross-Sell Targeting
**Description:** An RM reviews their portfolio and discovers a client with a gross wallet size of $50M but the bank only captures 15% share. The agent queries the Customer Wallet and KPI details table to identify top-ranked product buckets where the client spends externally. By cross-referencing the Account Planning report header, the agent confirms there are no risk constraints blocking new business. The agent then recommends specific products (trade finance, FX hedging, or cash management) based on the client's industry and current banking patterns. The agent should generate a prioritized action list with estimated revenue potential for each cross-sell opportunity.

### Scenario 2: Unused Credit Facility Activation
**Description:** The agent identifies a client with an approved but unutilized credit facility by analyzing the Account Planning report header (funded vs unfunded limits). By examining the Monthly profitability Aggregate, the agent determines the client is profitable on fee-based products but generates minimal interest income. The agent investigates whether market conditions or client liquidity have changed by checking recent KPI trends in the Customer Wallet table. If the client's business is growing (indicated by wallet expansion), the agent alerts the RM that the client may have shifted borrowing to competitors. The agent recommends scheduling a strategic review to understand why the facility remains unused and proposes competitive pricing or product restructuring.

### Scenario 3: Deposit Growth Campaign Targeting
**Description:** An RM needs to grow their deposit base to improve the bank's liquidity ratios. The agent queries the Monthly profitability Aggregate to identify clients with low or declining deposit balances but strong overall relationships. By analyzing margin percentages and net interest metrics, the agent calculates the opportunity cost of low deposit penetration. The agent then segments clients by profitability tier and recommends targeted campaigns for high-value clients with deposit potential. The agent should factor in the client's segment and banking type from the Customer Master Directory to personalize the deposit product offering (sweep accounts, yield-enhanced deposits, or transaction accounts).

### Scenario 4: Transaction Banking Penetration Gap
**Description:** The agent discovers a multinational client using the bank for lending but not for cash management or trade services. By querying the Group Geographic Presence Matrix, the agent identifies that the client operates in 12 countries where the bank has a strong transaction banking presence. The Monthly profitability Aggregate shows minimal revenue from transaction banking sub-lines despite significant credit exposure. The agent calculates the potential revenue uplift if the client consolidated their payment flows and trade finance needs with the bank. The RM is provided with a country-by-country opportunity map and recommended discussion points for the next client meeting.

### Scenario 5: Corporate Finance Advisory Upsell
**Description:** A long-standing client is showing signs of expansion based on wallet growth trends in the Customer Wallet and KPI details table. The agent cross-references recent call reports from Client Interaction logs and notices the client mentioned potential M&A activity. By checking the Account Planning report header, the agent confirms the client has strong credit ratings and capacity for advisory fees. The agent proactively alerts the RM to position the bank's corporate finance team for advisory services (M&A, debt restructuring, or capital raising). The agent includes a summary of the client's strategic direction, financial health, and the bank's existing relationship depth to strengthen the pitch.

### Scenario 6: FX and Derivatives Product Activation
**Description:** The agent identifies a client with significant international operations (from Group Geographic Presence Matrix) but zero revenue from FX or derivatives products in the Monthly profitability Aggregate. By analyzing the client's industry segment and wallet data, the agent estimates the client's hedging needs based on typical industry benchmarks. The agent checks whether the client has expressed FX risk concerns in recent call reports. If regulatory or internal risk ratings permit, the agent recommends the RM schedule a treasury advisory session to introduce FX forwards, options, or structured hedging solutions. The agent quantifies potential fee revenue and positions this as a risk management value-add rather than a sales push.

### Scenario 7: Fee-Based Product Replacement Strategy
**Description:** A client is generating strong interest income but minimal fee revenue, indicating over-reliance on credit products. The agent queries the Monthly profitability Aggregate to identify which fee-based product lines are underpenetrated (e.g., guarantees, letters of credit, or agency services). By reviewing the customer's segment and business model from the Customer Master Directory, the agent matches the client to relevant fee products. The agent also checks Account Planning narratives to ensure the proposed products align with the strategic direction. The RM receives a report showing how product diversification can stabilize revenue even if credit demand softens, along with case studies of similar clients who successfully adopted these products.

### Scenario 8: Annual Review Upsell Opportunity Identification
**Description:** As the annual account planning cycle approaches, the agent automatically generates a pre-meeting brief for each client relationship. By analyzing year-over-year changes in the Customer Wallet and KPI details, the agent highlights where the client's business has grown but the bank's share has remained flat or declined. The agent cross-references the Account Planning report header to review last year's strategic objectives and flags which targets were met, missed, or require revision. The Monthly profitability Aggregate is used to show product-by-product performance trends. The agent synthesizes this into a concise "Opportunities & Gaps" summary, enabling the RM to enter the review meeting with data-driven talking points and specific product recommendations.

### Scenario 9: Lost Business Recapture Campaign
**Description:** The agent detects a significant drop in a specific revenue line (e.g., trade finance) in the Monthly profitability Aggregate for a previously active client. By comparing current-year vs prior-year values in the Customer Wallet table, the agent confirms the client's overall wallet has not shrunk, meaning the business likely shifted to a competitor. The agent checks recent Client Interaction and Call Reports to determine if there were service issues, pricing disputes, or relationship friction. If the client relationship is otherwise healthy, the agent recommends the RM proactively reach out to understand the reason for the shift and propose a win-back strategy (competitive pricing, enhanced service levels, or product innovation). The agent includes competitive intelligence if available and suggests positioning improvements.

## Category 2: Risk & Exposure Management

### Scenario 21: Approaching Credit Limit Warning
**Description:** The agent continuously monitors the Account Planning report header for each client, comparing current exposure against funded and unfunded limits. When a client's total exposure reaches 85% of their approved limit, the agent triggers an automatic alert to the RM. By analyzing the Monthly profitability Aggregate, the agent determines whether the increased exposure is driven by increased borrowing or declining profitability (which affects risk-weighted assets). The agent also checks the internal/external ratings to see if credit quality has deteriorated. If limits are being exhausted but the client remains low-risk, the agent recommends initiating a limit increase request. If risk has increased, the agent suggests the RM focus on fee-based products and avoid further credit expansion until the next credit review.

### Scenario 22: Deteriorating Credit Rating Alert
**Description:** The agent detects a downgrade in a client's internal or external rating by monitoring changes in the Account Planning report header. The agent immediately assesses the impact on the bank's risk-weighted assets and regulatory capital by querying the Monthly profitability Aggregate. By reviewing the issues/action summary in the Account Planning table, the agent checks if the downgrade was anticipated or represents a new risk development. The agent then analyzes the Customer Wallet and KPI details to determine if the client's financial performance has weakened (declining wallet, reduced cross-sell flags). The RM receives a comprehensive risk brief including recommended actions: reduce exposure, increase pricing to reflect higher risk, request additional collateral, or intensify monitoring through more frequent call reports.

### Scenario 23: Concentration Risk in Single Sector
**Description:** An RM manages a portfolio heavily concentrated in a single industry sector (e.g., real estate, energy, or retail). The agent queries the Customer Master Directory and aggregates exposure by segment and sub-segment. When sector concentration exceeds internal risk thresholds, the agent triggers an alert. By analyzing the Monthly profitability Aggregate across all clients in that sector, the agent identifies which relationships are most profitable and which are marginal. The agent recommends the RM diversify their portfolio by targeting clients in under-represented sectors. The agent also monitors external market indicators (commodity prices, regulatory changes, or economic trends) and proactively alerts the RM if the concentrated sector faces systemic risks. This early warning enables portfolio rebalancing before regulatory scrutiny or credit losses materialize.

### Scenario 24: Collateral Valuation Review Trigger
**Description:** The agent monitors the Account Planning report header for clients with secured lending facilities. By tracking the approval status and exposure fields, the agent identifies when collateral was last revalued. If the revaluation date exceeds internal policy thresholds (e.g., 12-18 months for real estate or equipment), the agent flags the account for urgent collateral review. The agent also cross-references the Monthly profitability Aggregate to see if the client's financial performance has weakened, which might indicate deteriorating collateral value. By querying the issues/action summary, the agent checks if previous reviews noted declining asset quality. The RM receives a prioritized list of accounts requiring immediate collateral reassessment, along with recommended actions (site visits, third-party appraisals, or covenant discussions).

### Scenario 25: Covenant Breach Early Warning
**Description:** The agent analyzes financial ratios and KPI history from the Financial Ratio and KPI History table (referenced but not detailed in your schema) to detect trends that may lead to covenant breaches. For example, if a client's debt-to-equity ratio is deteriorating quarter-over-quarter, the agent predicts a potential covenant breach before it officially occurs. By querying the Account Planning report header, the agent reviews the specific covenant terms and calculates the margin before breach. The agent also checks the Monthly profitability Aggregate to see if declining profitability is driving the issue. The RM receives an early warning with scenario analysis (e.g., "if current trend continues, covenant breach likely in Q3") and recommended preemptive actions: restructure covenants, require additional guarantees, or work with the client on operational improvements to stabilize ratios.

### Scenario 26: Unfunded Commitment Risk Review
**Description:** A client has a large unfunded commitment (revolving credit facility, standby letter of credit, or guarantee) that remains undrawn for an extended period. The agent queries the Account Planning report header to calculate the ratio of unfunded to funded exposure. By analyzing the Customer Wallet and KPI details, the agent determines if the client's business is contracting (declining wallet, lower cross-sell activity), which might indicate they no longer need the facility. The agent also checks recent call reports to see if the client mentioned plans to draw down or cancel the facility. If the commitment is outdated, the agent recommends the RM propose a facility review to rightsize the commitment, freeing up regulatory capital and reducing contingent risk. This proactive management improves capital efficiency and maintains accurate risk exposure reporting.

### Scenario 27: Cross-Default Risk Assessment
**Description:** The agent detects a negative news event (legal dispute, regulatory action, or financial distress) affecting one client in a corporate group. By querying the Customer Master Directory (customer group and group key), the agent identifies all related entities within the group that also bank with the institution. The agent analyzes each entity's exposure in the Account Planning report header and calculates the aggregate group exposure. By reviewing the Monthly profitability Aggregate, the agent assesses which entities are profitable and which are marginal. The agent flags potential cross-default risk (where one entity's failure triggers defaults across the group) and recommends the RM coordinate with other relationship managers to develop a unified risk mitigation strategy. This prevents siloed decision-making and ensures comprehensive group-level risk management.

### Scenario 28: Sector-Specific Stress Testing
**Description:** The agent conducts proactive stress testing on clients in sectors facing headwinds (e.g., rising interest rates for real estate, commodity price shocks for energy, or regulatory changes for pharmaceuticals). By querying the Customer Master Directory, the agent segments the portfolio by sector and sub-segment. The agent then analyzes the Financial Ratio and KPI History to baseline each client's financial resilience. Using scenario modeling, the agent estimates the impact of adverse conditions (e.g., 20% revenue decline or 200bps interest rate increase) on each client's debt service coverage and covenant compliance. The RM receives a ranked list of vulnerable clients with recommended actions: increase monitoring frequency, require cash flow stress tests from the client, or reduce exposure preemptively. This forward-looking approach enables the bank to manage risk before problems materialize.

### Scenario 29: Regulatory Capital Optimization
**Description:** The agent analyzes the Monthly profitability Aggregate and identifies clients generating low returns on risk-weighted assets (RWA). By querying the Account Planning report header, the agent reviews each client's internal rating and exposure to calculate their capital consumption. The agent then compares this to the client's profitability and strategic importance. For clients with high RWA but low profitability, the agent recommends the RM re-negotiate terms (higher pricing, shorter tenor, or additional collateral) to improve risk-adjusted returns. Alternatively, if the client relationship is marginal, the agent suggests reducing exposure to free up regulatory capital for more profitable opportunities. This ensures the bank's capital is deployed efficiently and supports higher-value relationships.

The RM receives a country-by-country risk profile and actionable recommendations to protect the bank's exposure.

## Category 3: Profitability Analysis & Optimization

### Scenario 41: Margin Compression Trend Detection
**Description:** The agent monitors the Monthly profitability Aggregate and detects declining margin percentages for a specific client over consecutive quarters. By analyzing net interest and fee metrics, the agent identifies whether margin compression is driven by lower pricing, higher funding costs, or reduced product utilization. The agent queries the Customer Wallet and KPI details to determine if the client's overall wallet has expanded (suggesting the client is growing but the bank is losing share at lower margins). The agent also checks the Account Planning report header to see if competitive pressures or strategic pricing decisions caused the margin decline. The RM receives a margin analysis report with recommendations: renegotiate pricing at renewal, shift to higher-margin products, or accept lower margins if the client is strategically important and generates high volumes.

### Scenario 42: Unprofitable Client Identification
**Description:** The agent analyzes the Monthly profitability Aggregate to identify clients generating negative or near-zero profitability despite consuming significant relationship management time and credit capacity. By calculating return on equity (ROE) or return on assets (ROA) for each client, the agent ranks the portfolio by profitability. The agent queries the Customer Master Directory to check the client's segment and strategic importance. For clients that are unprofitable and non-strategic, the agent recommends the RM initiate a "fix or exit" strategy: increase pricing, reduce credit exposure, charge service fees, or gracefully exit the relationship. For strategically important clients, the agent identifies which products are dragging down profitability and recommends restructuring the relationship to improve margins while maintaining the strategic connection.

### Scenario 43: Product-Line Performance Benchmarking
**Description:** An RM wants to understand how their portfolio's product performance compares to peer RMs or the broader bank. The agent aggregates the Monthly profitability Aggregate across all clients and calculates average revenue per product line (transaction banking, corporate finance, markets, lending). By querying the User Access and coverage Mapping, the agent identifies peer RMs managing similar client segments. The agent then benchmarks the RM's performance against peers and highlights gaps: products where the RM underperforms peers may indicate cross-sell gaps or service delivery issues. The agent also identifies products where the RM outperforms, suggesting best practices that could be shared with other RMs. This comparative analysis drives continuous improvement and identifies coaching or training opportunities.

### Scenario 44: Cost-to-Serve Analysis
**Description:** The agent calculates the cost-to-serve for each client relationship by analyzing the time and resources consumed (from Client Interaction and Call Reports) relative to revenue generated (from Monthly profitability Aggregate). By querying the Customer Master Directory, the agent segments clients by size, complexity, and segment. The agent identifies clients with high cost-to-serve but low profitability, indicating inefficient resource allocation. For high-maintenance clients, the agent recommends the RM implement efficiency measures: reduce meeting frequency, transition to digital self-service channels, or assign a junior RM for day-to-day servicing. For high-value clients with justifiably high service needs, the agent ensures the cost is reflected in pricing or strategic importance. This analysis optimizes RM time allocation and improves portfolio-level profitability.

### Scenario 45: Fee Revenue Leakage Detection
**Description:** The agent monitors the Monthly profitability Aggregate for clients where transaction volumes are high but fee revenue is disproportionately low, indicating potential fee waivers, pricing errors, or missed billing. By querying the Detailed Monthly Profitability Ledger, the agent identifies specific fee line items that should have been charged but were not. The agent cross-references the Account Planning report header to check if fee waivers were negotiated as part of the relationship strategy. If no waiver was approved, the agent flags revenue leakage and recommends the RM investigate: are fees being waived operationally without approval, or is there a system configuration error? The RM receives a fee revenue recovery report with estimated lost revenue and recommended corrective actions, potentially recovering significant annual income.

### Scenario 46: Interest Income Optimization
**Description:** A client maintains large deposit balances but generates minimal interest income for the bank due to low or zero interest rates on their accounts. ## Implementation Guidance

### How to Use These Scenarios

Each scenario represents a specific situation where the AI agent should demonstrate reasoning capabilities, data synthesis, and actionable insights. To implement these scenarios:

1. **Knowledge Base Development:** Convert each scenario into training examples or knowledge base entries that teach the agent how to recognize similar situations in real-time data.

2. **Skill Creation:** For each scenario, develop a corresponding "skill" that codifies:
   - Trigger conditions (what data patterns activate this scenario)
   - Data sources required (which tables to query)
   - Analysis logic (how to process and synthesize the data)
   - Output format (what insights and recommendations to generate)
   - Action recommendations (what the RM should do)

3. **Prioritization:** Not all scenarios are equally important. Prioritize based on:
   - Frequency (how often does this situation occur?)
   - Impact (what is the revenue or risk implication?)
   - Feasibility (do you have the required data quality?)

4. **Iterative Development:** Start with 10-15 high-priority scenarios, validate agent performance, gather RM feedback, and gradually expand coverage.

5. **Continuous Learning:** As RMs interact with the agent, capture new scenarios and edge cases to continuously expand the agent's reasoning capabilities.
