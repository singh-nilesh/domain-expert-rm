# Research Plan: Scenario 2 - Unused Credit Facility Activation

## Scenario Summary
The RM identifies a client with an approved but unutilized credit facility. By analyzing profitability and market trends, the RM determines if the lack of use is due to liquidity changes, competitive displacement, or product misalignment. The goal is to propose restructuring or pricing strategies to encourage facility drawdown and increase interest income.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Understand how expert RMs diagnose the root cause of facility non-utilization and the strategies they use to "activate" dormant credit.
Secondary: Identify industry benchmarks for "utilization rates" in corporate banking, typical "commitment fee" structures, and competitive intelligence strategies for loan displacement.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do expert RMs interpret "Low Utilization"? Is it a sign of client strength (excess cash) or a sign of relationship failure (borrowing from competitors)?
2. **Decision Logic:** When should an RM recommend *reducing* a facility versus *restructuring* it to encourage use? What are the "activation triggers"?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What are the standard utilization benchmarks for Revolving Credit Facilities (RCF) vs. Term Loans in the current economic cycle?
4. **Calculations:** How do RMs calculate the "Negative Carry" or "Opportunity Cost" of an unused facility for both the bank (capital consumption) and the client (commitment fees)?
5. **Best Practices:** What are the most effective "win-back" or "activation" strategies (e.g., dynamic pricing, conversion to ESG-linked loans, or bridge-to-bond strategies)?
6. **Risk Management:** How does an unused facility affect the bank's Regulatory Capital (RWA) and Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)? What are the risks of "sudden drawdown"?
7. **Case Studies:** Examples of banks successfully activating dormant credit lines or right-sizing facilities to optimize capital.
