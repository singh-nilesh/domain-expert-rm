# Research Plan: Scenario 012 - Seasonal Business Financing Opportunity

## Scenario Summary
Seasonal businesses (Retail, Agriculture, Tourism, Construction) face predictable but acute liquidity stress during their "Peak-to-Trough" cycles. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) do not wait for a drawdown request; instead, they use historical borrowing patterns, industry-specific "Inventory Walls," and macroeconomic seasonal data to proactively propose financing solutions 60–90 days in advance. The goal is to ensure the bank captures 100% of the client's working capital needs while optimizing the bank's own liquidity planning.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for predicting and proactively financing seasonal business cycles.
Secondary: Identify specific "Seasonal Trigger" data points, flexible working capital structures (e.g., Borrowing Base, Seasonal Overlines), and industry-specific liquidity benchmarks.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "Seasonal Intelligence" Model:** How do expert RMs use historical data to "Model the Cycle" and predict the exact date of a client's liquidity gap?
2. **Industry-Specific Triggers:** What are the lead-lag indicators for different sectors (e.g., "Back-to-School" retail vs. "Harvest" agriculture)?
3. **Flexible Financing Structures:** What are the pros/cons of "Seasonal Overlines" vs. "Incremental Facility Tranches" vs. "Inventory Financing"?
4. **Risk Management in Seasonality:** How do RMs manage the "Repayment Risk" at the end of a season? What covenants (e.g., "Clean-down clauses") are standard?
5. **The Proactive Pitch:** How does an RM successfully position a financing proposal 90 days early without it feeling like "pushing debt"?
6. **Data Synthesis:** What specific fields in the core schema (Monthly Profitability Aggregate, KPI Details) are the most reliable for trend forecasting?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for a "Best-in-Class" seasonal banking relationship (e.g., Utilization Velocity, Early-Renewal Rate)?
