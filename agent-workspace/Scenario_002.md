# Scenario 002: Unused Credit Facility Activation

## Executive Summary
Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) do not view an unused credit facility as a "safe" or "passive" asset; rather, they see it as a **capital drag** that consumes regulatory capacity without generating interest income. In this scenario, the RM’s core mission is to diagnose the "why" behind the dormancy—distinguishing between a client holding a strategic liquidity buffer and a client who has displaced the bank with a competitor (e.g., Private Credit or Corporate Bonds). Success involves a "Three-R" strategy: **Re-Activating** the line through tactical triggers, **Right-Sizing** the commitment to optimize the bank's Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA), or **Re-Allocating** the capital to more profitable relationships.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** This scenario is triggered when a client's **Utilization Ratio** (Drawn / Limit) falls below 20% for consecutive quarters, or when the **Monthly Profitability Aggregate** shows a high ratio of fee-income-to-interest-income.
- **Stakeholders:** RM (Strategy), Credit Analyst (Covenant monitoring), Portfolio Manager (RWA/Capital optimization), and the Client’s Treasurer.
- **Challenge:** The "Cost of Liquidity." Under Basel III, undrawn committed lines require significant capital backing. If the client pays a 25 bps commitment fee but the bank's cost of capital is 12%, an unused line is a money-loser for the institution.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply a "Diagnostic-first" mental model to categorize the dormancy.

### Initial Assessment: The "Dormancy Diagnosis"
1. **Is it a "Rainy Day" Buffer?** (Client is conservative and values the "backstop" for M&A or volatility).
2. **Is it "Hidden Displacement"?** (Client is borrowing elsewhere—e.g., from a Private Credit fund—but keeps our line as a cheaper fallback).
3. **Is it "Operational Friction"?** (Client finds our drawdown process too manual or slow compared to rivals).
4. **Is it "Covenant Paralysis"?** (Client *cannot* draw because they are 5% away from a Leverage Ratio breach).

### Decision Logic: The "Three-R" Matrix
- **Re-Activate:** If the client has genuine funding needs but is using competitors. *Action: Pricing match, ESG-link conversion.*
- **Right-Size:** If the client has structurally excess cash and doesn't need the limit. *Action: Reduce limit, move to uncommitted status.*
- **Re-Allocate:** If the client is low-profitability and non-strategic. *Action: Non-renewal at maturity.*

## Expert Actions & Execution
Top RMs follow a structured activation pipeline:

### 1. Data Intelligence & Discovery
- **Benchmark Utilization:** Compare the client against peer averages (RCFs for Investment Grade are typically 10-20% utilized; Non-IG are 25-40%).
- **Analyze Payment Flows:** Scan the "Detailed Profitability Ledger" for payments to other financial institutions. Expert RMs look for recurring transfers to firms like **Apollo, Blackstone, or Ares**, which signal private credit displacement.

### 2. Identify "Activation Triggers"
- **Liquidity Stress Signals:** Look for declining average daily balances or a shift in payment patterns (e.g., client starts missing "2/10 Net 30" supplier discounts).
- **Growth Signals:** New supplier onboarding in foreign regions or spikes in transactional volume suggest a need for working capital.

### 3. Tactical Re-Structuring
- **ESG-Linked Conversion:** Offer to convert the unused RCF into a **Sustainability-Linked Loan (SLL)**. By tying the margin (and commitment fee) to ESG KPIs, the bank provides a "reason to draw" that aligns with the client's public sustainability goals.
- **"Bridge-to-Bond" Strategy:** Position the credit line as a bridge for an upcoming bond issuance.

### 4. Pricing & Fee Optimization
- **Commitment Fee Review:** If the client won't draw, ensure the **Commitment Fee** (industry standard: 25-100 bps) is at least 50% of the applicable margin to meet the bank's ROE hurdle.
- **Utilization-Based Pricing:** Propose a "Step-down" margin—if the client draws >50% of the line, the interest rate spread decreases by 25 bps.

### 5. Client Negotiation & Strategic Review
- **The "Use it or Lose it" Conversation:** Frame the limit reduction as a way for the client to save on commitment fees, while the bank re-allocates capital to "growing partners."

## Real-World Examples & Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Major U.S. Bank - Proactive Activation
- **Situation:** A national bank used predictive modeling to identify $5B in unutilized capacity across 500+ dormant accounts.
- **Action:** RMs were incentivized to offer "Drawdown Spreads" (discounted rates for the first 90 days) for specific CapEx projects.
- **Outcome:** The bank saw a **$700 Million uplift** in proactive credit line increases/activations and a **50% gain in processing efficiency**.

### Case Study 2: European Bank - ESG Conversion
- **Situation:** A manufacturing client kept a €100M revolver undrawn for 2 years.
- **Action:** The RM proposed an SLL conversion with 5 bps margin ratchets tied to carbon reduction targets.
- **Outcome:** The client drew down €40M to fund a green factory retrofit, converting a dormant "capital drag" into an active, interest-bearing ESG asset with high "stickiness."

### Case Study 3: SME Re-Activation Engine
- **Situation:** CrediaBank (2026) targeted 800k+ "Inactive" SME accounts.
- **Action:** Implemented a "Human-to-Human" model where RMs re-engaged dormant SMEs with tailored working capital products.
- **Outcome:** Achieved a **20-25% increase** in activated credit reserves and **25% revenue growth** from the previously dormant portfolio.

## Industry Best Practices
- **Active Balance Sheet Steering:** Treat undrawn lines as "lazy capital." Leading banks (BCG research) use AI to predict drawdown probability and adjust HQLA (High-Quality Liquid Assets) accordingly.
- **Uncommitted vs. Committed:** For "just in case" liquidity, push clients toward **Uncommitted Facilities**. These carry a **0% CCF** (Credit Conversion Factor) in many jurisdictions, significantly lowering the bank's capital requirement.
- **Level 3 Data Enrichment:** Analyze line-item supplier data. If a client is paying for "raw steel" but not using their credit line to hedge commodity risk, there is an activation opportunity.

## Client Value Proposition
- **Predictability:** "By activating this line for your Q3 inventory build, you lock in a cost of capital of SOFR + 200 bps, insulating you from potential market volatility in the commercial paper market."
- **Cost Savings:** "Right-sizing your facility from $100M to $60M will save you **$100,000 annually** in unnecessary commitment fees."

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **Capital Analytics Platforms:** Tools to calculate **RAROC** and **RWA Impact** of unused lines.
- **Predictive Liquidity Models:** AI that flags "likelihood to draw" based on cash flow volatility.
- **SLLP (Sustainability-Linked Loan Principles):** Framework for structuring ESG-linked activations.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **Sudden Drawdown (Liquidity Risk):** Monitor **LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio)**. Undrawn corporate lines assume a **10-30% outflow rate** in stress tests.
- **Covenant Monitoring:** Ensure dormant clients haven't become "silent risks." An unused line can be a sign the client is "locked out" of borrowing due to technical breaches.
- **Negative Carry:** For the bank, holding HQLA against an unused line often yields less than the bank's cost of funds.
