# Research Plan: Scenario 4 - Transaction Banking Penetration Gap

## Scenario Summary
The RM identifies a client with a significant lending relationship but minimal usage of the bank’s transaction banking services (payments, liquidity, cash management). By analyzing transaction data and identifying "held-away" flows, the RM develops a strategy to capture the high-margin, fee-based transaction business. The goal is to move the "Primary Operating Account" and deepen the relationship "moat."

## Research Objectives
Primary: Understand the step-by-step process expert RMs use to identify "transactional leakage" and the value-based arguments used to win the transaction business.
Secondary: Identify industry benchmarks for "Product Attachment Rates," typical "Transaction-to-Loan" revenue ratios, and the role of API/ERP integration in closing the penetration gap.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do expert RMs view the relationship between Lending and Transaction Banking? (The "Anchor vs. Engine" model).
2. **Decision Logic:** What specific signals (e.g., wire volumes to rivals) indicate a "Penetration Gap"? How do experts prioritize which transaction products to pitch first?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What is the average "Transaction Banking Fee-to-Lending Interest" ratio for top-tier corporate relationships?
4. **Calculations:** How do RMs calculate the **Switching Cost** for a client and the **Incremental ROE** for the bank when transaction banking is added?
5. **Best Practices:** What are the most effective "bundling" or "relationship pricing" strategies to incentivize transaction banking adoption?
6. **Risk Management:** How does transaction banking data improve the bank's "Early Warning System" for credit risk?
7. **Case Studies:** Examples of banks successfully converting "Loan-Only" clients into "Full-Service" transaction banking clients.
