# Research Plan: Scenario 011 - M&A Lead Generation from Advisory Fees

## Scenario Summary
This scenario focuses on the Relationship Manager's (RM) ability to transition from a "Lender" to a "Strategic Advisor" by identifying M&A triggers through advisory-led dialogue. Expert RMs monitor client expansion signals—such as wallet growth, strategic mentions in call reports, and strong credit capacity—to position the bank's corporate finance team. The goal is to capture high-margin advisory fees and lead-left financing mandates by being the first to identify and support the client's inorganic growth strategy.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the "Advisory-to-Mandate" pipeline used by top-tier RMs to uncover and secure M&A leads.
Secondary: Identify specific "Strategic Triggers," advisory fee benchmarks, and the internal coordination model between the RM and the Investment Banking/Corporate Finance teams.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "Advisory First" Mindset:** How do expert RMs structure strategic dialogues to move beyond credit needs and uncover M&A intent?
2. **Expansion Signals:** What specific data points (wallet growth, KPI shifts, industry trends) are the most reliable indicators that a client is preparing for an acquisition?
3. **The "Pitch" Architecture:** How does an RM successfully position a "Corporate Finance" specialist without appearing to just "sell another product"?
4. **Advisory Fee Benchmarks:** What are the industry-standard success fees and retainer models for middle-market vs. large corporate advisory?
5. **Internal Coordination:** What does the "Hand-off" or "Joint-Coverage" model look like between the RM and the M&A team? How is revenue shared?
6. **Value-Add Frameworks:** What proprietary frameworks (e.g., "M&A Readiness Scorecard") do top banks use to add value before a deal is even live?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for an "Advisory-Led" relationship (e.g., Advisory Fee/Lending Revenue ratio, M&A Pipeline Velocity)?
