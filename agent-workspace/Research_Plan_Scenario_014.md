# Research Plan: Scenario 014 - Supply Chain Finance Penetration

## Scenario Summary
Supply Chain Finance (SCF), often referred to as "Reverse Factoring," is a strategic tool that allows a large buyer to optimize its working capital while providing its suppliers with access to low-cost liquidity. Expert RMs identify SCF opportunities by analyzing a client's "Payables Profile"—identifying large, fragmented supplier bases and extended payment terms. By pitching an SCF program, the RM helps the buyer extend their Days Payable Outstanding (DPO) without hurting supplier relationships, while the bank captures high-volume, low-risk transaction revenue. The goal is to move from a "Lender to the Buyer" to an "Orchestrator of the Ecosystem."

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for identifying, pitching, and implementing an SCF program.
Secondary: Identify specific "Payables Signals," SCF pricing models (Arbitrage between Buyer and Supplier credit ratings), and best practices for supplier onboarding.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "Payables Audit" Framework:** How do expert RMs analyze a client's payables ledger to identify "SCF-ready" supplier clusters?
2. **The ROI of SCF for Buyers:** How do RMs quantify the impact of DPO extension on a buyer's free cash flow and ROE?
3. **The ROI of SCF for Suppliers:** How is the "Liquidity Premium" calculated for small suppliers accessing the buyer's higher credit rating?
4. **Data Signals for SCF:** What transaction patterns (e.g., spike in "Early Payment" requests, high volume of cross-border payables) indicate a need for SCF?
5. **The "ESG-Linked SCF" Trend:** How are banks integrating Sustainability KPIs into SCF pricing to drive green supply chain behavior?
6. **Supplier Onboarding Best Practices:** What are the most common barriers to supplier adoption (e.g., KYC friction) and how do experts solve them?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for a "High-Performing" SCF program (e.g., Supplier Participation Rate, Program Utilization %, Revenue per Supplier)?
