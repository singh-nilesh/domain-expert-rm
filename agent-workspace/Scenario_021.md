# Scenario 021: Product Bundle Discount Strategy

## Executive Summary
In the commoditized arena of corporate lending, "Price" has historically been the primary weapon for client acquisition, leading to a "race to the bottom" that erodes institutional profitability. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) disrupt this trend by utilizing **Product Bundle Discounts**—a strategic, data-driven pricing model where the cost of the "Anchor" product (e.g., a revolving credit facility) is dynamically linked to the client’s adoption of high-margin, capital-light ancillaries. This scenario focuses on the transition from "Deal-by-Deal" pricing to **Relationship-Based Pricing (RBP).** By structuring a multi-tiered discount grid that rewards "Wallet Consolidation," the RM transforms the bank from a transactional lender into a **Strategic Primary Partner.** The financial impact is profound: while a standalone loan often yields a sub-optimal ROE (frequently <5%), a bundled relationship can swing that return to **11.6% or higher**, simply by capturing the "Side Business" of treasury, deposits, and FX.

The strategic mission in this scenario is to move the conversation from "Line-Item" pricing to **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO).** Expert RMs use "Conditional Pricing Covenants"—legal clauses in the credit agreement that adjust the interest spread based on actual ancillary volume—to eliminate "Price Leakage." This ensures that if a client takes the "Relationship Discount" but fails to move their primary operating accounts, the loan rate automatically "steps up" to reflect its standalone risk. Furthermore, in the era of **Basel III Endgame**, where Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA) are at a premium, bundling allows the bank to "subsidize" capital-heavy lending with high-margin fee revenue, effectively "de-risking" the bank's own P&L while providing the client with a lower all-in cost of capital.

Successful execution requires a sophisticated understanding of **Anti-Tying Regulations** (e.g., Section 106 of the BHCA). Expert RMs navigate these rules by always offering a "Meaningful Option" for standalone pricing, ensuring the discount is positioned as a voluntary incentive rather than a coercive requirement. Ultimately, this scenario is about **"Strategic Entrenchment"**—using the pricing grid to "lock in" the client for the long term. Research shows that clients using 4+ bundled products have a **50% lower churn rate** than those with a single operating account, creating a "fortress" around the relationship that competitors cannot easily penetrate.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** 
    - **Competitive:** A Tier 1 client is being poached by a rival offering a "loss-leader" rate on a standalone loan.
    - **Data-Driven:** The **Monthly Profitability Aggregate** shows a "Fee-to-Interest Ratio" below 0.2x, indicating the bank is only a "Secondary" provider.
    - **Cyclical:** The approach of a major facility renewal, providing the "Lever" to renegotiate the total relationship mix.
- **Stakeholders:** 
    - **Internal:** RM (Lead), Pricing Desk / Profitability Committee, Product Specialists (GTS, FX), Legal (Anti-Tying Compliance).
    - **External:** CFO (Decision Maker), Treasurer (Negotiator), Procurement (Benchmarking).
- **The Challenge:** "The Cherry Picker." Clients who want the bank’s "best-in-market" loan rate but want to keep their "best-in-market" FX rate at a boutique rival.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply the **"Total Relationship Value (TRV)"** mental model.

### 1. The "Anchor & Add-on" Logic
The RM views the product suite as a hierarchy of value:
- **The Anchor (Lending):** High RWA usage, low margin, high competitive pressure. **Purpose:** To "Buy" the right to the wallet.
- **The Add-ons (Fees):** Zero/Low RWA, high margin, recurring revenue. **Purpose:** To "Earn" the relationship ROE.
- **Action:** Never discount the Anchor without a legally documented commitment to the Add-ons.

### 2. The "Step-Down" Margin Formula
The RM calculates how many "bps" of loan spread each ancillary is worth:
- **Cash Management Mandate:** Worth **10–15 bps** of spread (due to low-cost deposits).
- **50% FX Mandate:** Worth **5–10 bps** of spread (high-margin fees).
- **Trade Finance (LCs):** Worth **5 bps** of spread (RWA offset).
- **Strategic Rule:** The total discount must never exceed 50% of the incremental fee revenue gained.

### 3. Decision Logic: "Relationship Discount" vs. "Tying"
To remain compliant, the RM follows the **"Meaningful Option"** test:
- **Prohibited (Tying):** "We will only provide this loan if you use our FX desk."
- **Permissible (Bundling):** "Our standard loan rate is SOFR + 250bps. However, for our 'Strategic Partners' who use us for Treasury and FX, we offer a preferred rate of SOFR + 215bps."

## Expert Actions & Execution: The Bundling Pipeline

### Phase 1: The "Profitability Baseline" (Discovery)
The agent aggregates data to simulate the "Relationship Swap":
1.  **RAROC Simulation:** *"If we keep the loan rate at 250bps, our ROE is 8%. If we drop to 215bps but capture $50M in FX volume, our ROE jumps to 16%. We have a 'Pricing Cushion' of 35bps to win this deal."*
2.  **Wallet Sizing:** Identify exactly which competitors currently hold the "Add-ons."

### Phase 2: The "Total Cost" Pitch (Talk Tracks)
The RM avoids the "Line-item battle." They lead with **TCO Insights**:
- **The "Strategic Iceberg" Track:** *"You're looking at the 10bps difference in the loan rate—that’s the tip of the iceberg. Below the surface is the $150k you're losing every year in manual reconciliation and idle cash buffers because your data is spread across 4 banks. Let's look at the 'All-in' cost."*
- **The "Modular ROI" Track:** *"We can start with the 'Treasury Plus' bundle. The 25bps spread discount you'll receive today will effectively 'fund' your ERP integration costs for the next 6 months."*

### Phase 3: Structuring the "Pricing Grid"
The RM presents a formal **Relationship Pricing Matrix** (see below):
- **Level 1 (Bronze):** Loan only. Price = Market Rate.
- **Level 2 (Silver):** Loan + Operating Account. Price = Market - 15bps.
- **Level 3 (Gold):** Loan + Operating + FX + Trade. Price = Market - 35bps.

### Phase 4: Implementation of "Pricing Covenants"
- **The Enforcement Clause:** Ensure the Credit Memo states: *"Pricing is contingent on a minimum Average Daily Balance (ADB) of $5M. If ADB falls below threshold for 2 consecutive quarters, spread increases by 25bps."*
- **Automated Monitoring:** The agent monitors the **Detailed Ledger** quarterly to flag "Price Compliance" to the RM.

## Real-World Case Studies

### Case Study 1: The "Treasury Swap" Success
- **Situation:** A $250M revenue importer was using Bank A for a loan and Bank B for Treasury. Bank A’s relationship was loss-making (4% ROE).
- **Action:** The RM offered to **refinance the $30M loan at a 25bps discount** ONLY if the client moved the "Main Operating Account" (MOA) and FX flow.
- **Outcome:** Client moved 100% of the wallet.
- **Result:** Relationship ROE jumped from **4% to 18%**. The low-cost deposits from the MOA more than offset the loan discount.

### Case Study 2: JPMorgan's "One Chase" Bundling
- **Example:** JPM offers mortgage rate discounts of up to **1.00%** for clients who move $300k+ in "New Money" to their investment/deposit accounts.
- **Impact:** Achieved a **10-point improvement in NPS** (to 65) by rewarding consolidation.
- **Result:** Existing customer profitability grew by **40% in a single year** for targeted bundled segments.

### Case Study 3: The "Pricing Covenant" Defensive Play
- **Situation:** A client took a "Gold" discount but quietly moved their FX business back to a fintech rival.
- **Action:** The RM’s dashboard flagged the "Fee Leakage." The RM invoked the **Pricing Covenant**, increasing the loan spread by 25bps.
- **Outcome:** The client realized the "hidden cost" and moved the FX business back to the bank to regain the discount.

## Industry Best Practices & Benchmarks
- **The "Rule of 3+":** Churn rate drops from **25% (1 product) to 11% (4+ products).**
- **SOW Target:** Expert RMs aim to capture **>60% SOW** for Tier 1 clients through bundling.
- **Discount Cap:** Standalone-to-Gold discount should rarely exceed **40–50 bps** to maintain capital integrity.
- **Implementation Speed:** API-led "Bundle Onboarding" should take **<14 days** to ensure the client sees immediate value.
- **Lead Yield:** Solution bundling increases conversion rates by **up to 50%** compared to a-la-carte pitches.

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **Relationship Pricing Engines:** Tools like *PrecisionLender, Zafin,* or *Q2* for real-time RAROC simulation.
- **TCO ROI Calculators:** Internal models used to quantify "Manual Labor Costs" vs. "Integrated Banking."
- **Anti-Tying Playbooks:** Legal "Green/Yellow/Red" guides for RMs to ensure compliant pitching.
- **Next Best Action (NBA) Dashboards:** To identify which "Add-on" will have the highest ROE lift for a specific client.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **"Revenue Cannibalization" Risk:** Monitor the "All-in" margin to ensure you aren't discounting a large loan for a tiny ancillary.
- **Operational Complexity:** Bundling increases the "Inter-dependency" of departments. Ensure the Treasury team can handle the surge in onboarding after a "Gold" deal is won.
- **Basel IV Compliance:** Monitor the RWA impact of "Unused Commitments" in bundled facilities, which can spike capital costs if not structured as uncommitted.
