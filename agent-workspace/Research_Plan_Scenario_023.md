# Research Plan: Scenario 023 - Deteriorating Credit Rating Alert

## Scenario Summary
A credit rating downgrade is a seismic event in the relationship lifecycle. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) do not view a downgrade as a simple compliance update; they treat it as an **Urgent Risk Diagnostic** event. This scenario focuses on the immediate assessment of a rating shift—whether internal or external (e.g., Moody’s, S&P)—and its cascading impact on the bank’s regulatory capital and the client’s facility pricing. By synthesizing "Issues/Action" logs, recent KPI trends, and macroeconomic headwinds, the RM develops a "Risk Triage Plan" that ranges from "Increased Monitoring" to "Exposure Reduction" or "Collateral Hardening." The goal is to stabilize the relationship and protect the bank's RWA efficiency while providing the client with a path back to a "Stable" outlook.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for responding to a deteriorating credit rating.
Secondary: Identify the specific "RWA Inflation" impact of downgrades, "Covenant Hardening" techniques, and the best practices for conducting a "Risk-Pricing" renegotiation.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "RWA Inflation" Mechanic:** How does a 1-notch vs. 2-notch downgrade affect the bank's regulatory capital charge for a typical corporate loan?
2. **Identifying the "True" Cause:** How do expert RMs distinguish between a "Technical Downgrade" (e.g., sector-wide shift) and a "Fundamental Downgrade" (e.g., operational failure)?
3. **The "Covenant Hardening" Menu:** What specific tighter covenants (e.g., "Equity Cure" restrictions, "Asset Disposal" blocks) are standard in a downgrade response?
4. **Risk-Pricing Adjustments:** What are the industry-standard "Step-up" margins for downgraded clients? How does an RM pitch a higher rate to a struggling client?
5. **The "Exit vs. Support" Decision Tree:** What data signals (Liquidity Runway, Management Quality, Strategic Importance) determine if a bank should stay and "work it out" or "exit"?
6. **Internal Alignment (Special Assets):** At what rating threshold does an RM lose control of the account to the "Special Assets" or "Workout" team?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for a "Successful Risk Mitigation" (e.g., LGD reduction, Time-to-Stabilize, Fee-to-Loss ratio)?
