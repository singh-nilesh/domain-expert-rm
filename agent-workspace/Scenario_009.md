# Scenario 009: Lost Business Recapture Campaign

## Executive Summary
In corporate banking, "Lost Business" is rarely an overnight event; it is a gradual process of **"Silent Attrition."** Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) view a departing client not as a permanent loss, but as a high-potential **"Strategic Re-entry"** opportunity. Because it is **5x cheaper** to win back a former client than to acquire a new one, and won-back clients often exhibit **double the lifetime value (LTV)**, the recapture campaign is a primary driver of portfolio ROE. This scenario focuses on using data-driven triggers to identify churn early, conduct surgical root-cause analysis, and deploy "Painless Transition" proposals that restore the bank to "Primary" status.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** 
    - **Quantitative:** A >25% drop in transaction volume (ACH/Wires) in the **Monthly Profitability Aggregate**.
    - **Transactional:** Spikes in transfers to competitor Routing Transit Numbers (RTNs).
    - **Contractual:** Inquiries regarding UCC-1 release or lien termination.
    - **Strategic:** Arrival of a new CFO with historical ties to a rival institution.
- **Stakeholders:** 
    - **Internal:** RM (Lead), Treasury Specialist, Implementation Taskforce (to de-risk the "switch-back").
    - **External:** Corporate Treasurer (Primary), CFO (Decision Maker), Procurement (Pricing).
- **The Challenge:** The "Hassle Factor." The biggest barrier to winning back a client is their memory of the operational friction that led them to leave or the perceived "work" required to switch back.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply the **"Churn Anatomy"** mental model to determine the "Recapture ROI."

### 1. The Churn Anatomy Matrix
The RM segments lost business to prioritize effort:
- **Price Switchers (Low Loyalty):** Left for a 5bps rate difference. **Action:** Only recapture if they have high cross-sell potential (e.g., Treasury fees). Avoid "Price Wars."
- **Service Switchers (High Potential):** Left due to a specific error or neglect. **Action:** **High Priority.** These are the easiest to win back with a "Service Recovery" play.
- **Product Switchers (Strategic):** Left because the bank lacked a specific tool (e.g., API integration). **Action:** Recapture only after the bank has launched the missing feature.

### 2. The "Relationship Velocity" Framework
The RM looks for "Silent Attrition" signals before the account is closed:
- **Velocity Drop:** Is the client logging in less frequently? Are they using the FX portal only for small, "odd-lot" trades?
- **Leakage Detection:** Is the "Main Operating Account" (MOA) status shifting? (e.g., Payroll files moving from $1M/month to $100k/month).

### 3. Decision Logic: The "Recapture Audit"
Before initiating a campaign, the RM asks:
- *"Was the client profitable (RAROC > 15%) before they left?"*
- *"Does the client still fit our current risk appetite (e.g., ESG standards)?"*
- *"Can we fix the root cause of their departure (e.g., new tech, new RM)?"*

## Expert Actions & Execution: The Recapture Playbook

### Phase 1: Silent Attrition Detection (The "Early Warning")
The agent monitors the **Monthly Profitability Aggregate** for "Negative Anomalies":
- **RTN Spikes:** Flag every transfer >$50k to a rival bank’s RTN.
- **Balance Decay:** Alert if Average Daily Balances (ADB) drop 30% below the 12-month rolling average.
- **Feature Abandonment:** Track if a previously active "sticky" feature (e.g., Positive Pay) is no longer being used.

### Phase 2: Root Cause Analysis (The "Exit Interview")
Instead of a generic sales call, the RM conducts a "Discovery Audit":
- **The CRM Audit:** Review past **Call Reports** for "Warning Phrases" (e.g., *"Portal is slow," "Pricing is non-competitive," "Delayed loan approval"*).
- **The "Truth Call":** *"We noticed a shift in your transaction patterns. I’m not here to sell; I’m here to understand where we fell short so I can fix it for my other clients."* This humility often lowers the client's guard.

### Phase 3: The "Pattern Interrupt" Outreach
Experts avoid "I miss you" emails. They lead with a **"Pattern Interrupt"**:
- **The Innovation Play:** *"Since we last worked together, we’ve invested $10M in our API-first Treasury portal. I’d like to show you how our new 'Liquidity Forecasting' tool addresses the exact manual pain point you mentioned last year."*
- **The Executive Outreach:** Have the Regional Head or C-Suite executive reach out to the client’s CFO to signal that this relationship is a "Top 10" priority for the bank.

### Phase 4: The Win-Back Proposal (The "Painless Transition")
The proposal must address the "Hassle Factor" head-on:
- **Dedicated Taskforce:** Offer a "White-Glove" implementation team to handle ERP integration and file mapping.
- **Economic Incentive:** A "Welcome Back" credit (e.g., 12 months of waived Treasury fees) to offset the legal and operational costs of switching back.
- **The "Safety Net" Pilot:** Propose moving a single "low-risk" subsidiary back first to "proof-of-concept" the new service model.

## Real-World Case Studies

### Case Study 1: The "Silent Churn" Recovery (Global Bank)
- **Situation:** A $500M manufacturer was gradually moving its FX business to a fintech rival.
- **Detection:** The RM’s dashboard flagged a 40% drop in FX volume despite a 10% increase in total revenue.
- **Action:** The RM pitched a new "Integrated FX" platform that embedded directly into the client’s SAP system.
- **Outcome:** Recaptured **$200M in annual FX flow** and restored "Primary" status.
- **Result:** $150k in annual fee income restored + 1,500% ROI on the campaign.

### Case Study 2: Service Recovery in Transaction Banking
- **Situation:** A client left due to a "neglect" period following an RM departure.
- **Action:** The new RM initiated an "Executive Re-commitment" call with the Head of Corporate Banking.
- **Outcome:** Won back the **Main Operating Account (MOA)** within 6 months.
- **Result:** $25M in new deposits and a 50-point increase in the client's NPS score.

### Case Study 3: The "Why" Campaign (Regional Bank)
- **Situation:** A bank automated alerts for all account closures >$10k.
- **Action:** RMs were required to call within 48 hours to ask "Why?".
- **Outcome:** Successfully "saved" 1.5% of departing clients (1,600 accounts).
- **Result:** Retained **$30M+ in asset value** that would have otherwise been lost to "silent attrition."

## Proactive "Look-alike" Churn Prevention
Expert RMs use a "Lost Business" event to protect the rest of their portfolio:
1. **Identify the "Churn Profile":** If a Manufacturing client left because of "Portal Speed," the RM identifies all other Manufacturing clients in the portfolio.
2. **The "Stay Interview":** Conduct a proactive "Value Review" with those clients *before* they show signs of decay.
3. **The "Pre-emptive Strike":** Offer the same "Welcome Back" incentives (e.g., fee credits) to at-risk clients as a "Loyalty Reward."

## Industry Best Practices & Benchmarks
- **Recapture Success Rate:** Aim for a **20-30% win-back rate** for high-value clients.
- **Churn Accuracy:** Top-tier predictive models (AA Workbenches) identify churn with **>70% accuracy**.
- **Retention ROI:** Retention initiatives generate **70% higher ROI** than new customer acquisition.
- **The "Rule of 4":** Relationships with 4+ products are **10x harder to displace** than single-product accounts.
- **"Speed to Lead":** Following up on a "Silent Attrition" signal within **5 days** increases recapture probability by 3x.

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **Predictive Churn Models:** ML-driven alerts based on transaction and behavioral data.
- **RTN Scrapers:** Automated monitoring of outbound wire/ACH routing numbers.
- **UCC Filing Alerts:** (e.g., *CreditRiskMonitor*) to track if a competitor is filing liens against your client.
- **ERP Integration Kits:** To facilitate "Painless Transitions" back to the bank.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **KYC/AML Refresh:** Winning back a former client requires a full "New-to-Bank" compliance check if the account has been closed for >12 months.
- **Non-Solicitation Clauses:** Ensure the recapture campaign does not violate any legal agreements if the client moved due to an RM jumping to a competitor.
- **Profitability Guardrails:** Do not use "Zero-Pricing" to win back business if it results in a negative RAROC. Focus on **Value** over **Price**.
