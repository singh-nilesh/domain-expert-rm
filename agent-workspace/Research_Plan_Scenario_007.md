# Research Plan: Scenario 7 - Fee-Based Product Replacement Strategy

## Scenario Summary
A client generates strong interest income (lending-heavy) but minimal fee revenue. This indicates an over-reliance on credit products, which are capital-intensive. The RM must identify and pitch fee-based products (Guarantees, Letters of Credit, Agency Services, Treasury Fees) that align with the client's business model to stabilize and diversify revenue.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Document how expert RMs transition "Lending-only" clients into "Fee-generative" relationships without damaging the credit partnership.
Secondary: Identify industry benchmarks for "Fee-to-Interest" ratios by sector, the ROE impact of fee-based products vs. lending, and specific "fee-hooks" for different corporate business models.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do RMs frame the transition from "Interest" to "Fees"? (The "Stability" vs. "Cost" argument).
2. **Decision Logic:** Which fee-based products have the highest "Right to Win" based on specific lending types (e.g., Asset-Based Lending vs. Cash Flow Lending)?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What is the target "Non-Interest Income (NII) to Total Revenue" percentage for a healthy corporate relationship?
4. **Calculations:** How do RMs demonstrate the "Cost-Benefit" of fee-based services (e.g., how an LC can reduce the need for a cash deposit or a more expensive loan)?
5. **Best Practices:** What are the best "Agency Services" or "Admin Fees" that are standard in the industry but often overlooked by RMs?
6. **Risk Management:** How does increasing fee-based business affect the bank's RWA and Capital hurdles compared to expanding a loan?
7. **Case Studies:** Examples of "Fee-Lite" relationships that were successfully "Fee-Activated" and the resulting impact on Relationship ROE.
