# Scenario 017: Dormant Account Reactivation

## Executive Summary
In the complex ecosystem of corporate banking, dormant accounts represent more than just a lack of revenue; they are **"Balance Sheet Anchor Drags"** that consume disproportionate amounts of KYC resources, regulatory capital, and relationship management time. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) view a portfolio of dormant accounts as a hidden reservoir of untapped value. This scenario focuses on the strategic imperative of **Dormant Account Reactivation**—the systematic process of identifying previously active clients who have ceased transaction activity and deploying data-driven "Re-engagement Playbooks" to restore them to profitability. In an era of high interest rates and compressed margins, reactivating a known, vetted client is **5x–10x more capital-efficient** than acquiring a new prospect, as the heavy lifting of onboarding and credit assessment has already been completed.

The strategic mission in this scenario is to conduct a surgical **"Dormancy Diagnostic"** that distinguishes between "Seasonal Hibernation" (temporary lulls in activity) and "Silent Churn" (where the client has mentally or operationally migrated to a competitor). An expert RM uses a "Three-Layer Analysis"—Behavioral, Operational, and Relationship signals—to uncover the "Breaking Point" that led to disengagement. Successful execution of this scenario requires the RM to adopt a **"Fix or Exit"** mindset. For clients that no longer align with the bank’s "Strategic Precision" (e.g., non-target sectors or low-ROE returners), the RM initiates a graceful exit to free up Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA) for higher-performing relationships. Ultimately, this scenario is about **"Portfolio Hygiene"**—ensuring that every client on the bank’s books is either a contributing partner or an active target for growth, thereby maximizing the overall portfolio Return on Equity (ROE).

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** 
    - **Quantitative:** No customer-initiated transactions (ACH, Wires, FX) for >180 days in the **Detailed Monthly Profitability Ledger**.
    - **Behavioral:** A "Login-only" pattern detected—the client logs into the portal to check balances but hasn't transacted in 3 consecutive months.
    - **Compliance-Driven:** Account is approaching a "KYC Refresh" deadline, but current revenue doesn't justify the cost of the compliance audit.
- **Stakeholders:** 
    - **Internal:** RM (Lead), Portfolio Manager (Hygiene Watchdog), Credit Risk (Exit Approver), Compliance (KYC Specialist).
    - **External:** CFO (Decision Maker), Treasurer (Operational Lead), Controller.
- **The Challenge:** "The Neglect Barrier." The RM often feels hesitant to call a client they haven't spoken to in a year, fearing a negative reaction to the bank's silence.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply a **"Diagnostic & Allocation"** mental model.

### 1. The Dormancy Diagnostic Matrix
The RM maps observable data to the likely "Reason for Silence":
- **Signal:** Sudden exit transfer followed by zero volume. **Root Cause:** Competitive Loss (moved to a rival for better rates/tech).
- **Signal:** Declining login frequency + high support ticket volume. **Root Cause:** Service Failure (frustration with the platform).
- **Signal:** Zero activity but associated credit line is paid on time. **Root Cause:** Project Completion (the account was for a specific, now-finished purpose).
- **Signal:** Change in primary signatory/CFO in CRM. **Root Cause:** Stakeholder Turnover (new leader prefers their old bank).

### 2. The "Fix or Exit" Decision Tree
Before picking up the phone, the RM asks three "Gatekeeper" questions:
1.  **Strategic Alignment:** Does this client fit our 2025 "Target Segment" (e.g., ESG-focused, High-Growth Tech)?
2.  **Profitability Potential:** If reactivated to "Primary" status, would their SOW yield an ROE > 12%?
3.  **Cost-to-Serve:** Is the manual effort of "Fixing" the relationship lower than the cost of a full "New-to-Bank" acquisition?
- **Action:** If "No" to any two, initiate the **Exit Protocol**. If "Yes," deploy the **Reactivation Playbook**.

### 3. The "Incentive Ladder" Logic
The RM chooses the "Hook" based on the client's past behavior:
- **Price-Sensitive:** Pitch a 6-month "Fee Holiday" on all domestic payments.
- **Efficiency-Seeker:** Pitch "Free ERP Integration" subsidy ($5k-$10k credit toward technical setup).
- **Advisory-Led:** Pitch a "Complimentary Treasury Health Check" or industry benchmarking report.

## Expert Actions & Execution: The Reactivation Playbook

### Phase 1: Pre-Outreach Intelligence (The "Clean Record")
1.  **LinkedIn Audit:** Verify if the Treasurer is still the same. If not, research the new hire’s background.
2.  **Compliance Path-finding:** Meet with the KYC specialist to identify the *minimum* documentation needed to make the account "Transaction-Ready." Do not lead with a "compliance chore."

### Phase 2: The "Value-Led" Outreach (The Reset)
The RM avoids the "Just checking in" call. They lead with **Insights**:
- **Scenario A (New Stakeholder):** *"Welcome to the role. I’ve prepared a 'State of the Relationship' report showing how we’ve supported your predecessor and identifying $50k in potential savings through our new API Hub."*
- **Scenario B (Service Recovery):** *"I understand we had some portal outages last year that caused your team frustration. I’m calling because we’ve moved to a new Agentic AI architecture that has reduced processing errors by 90%. I’d like to prove it with a 30-day pilot."*

### Phase 3: The "Frictionless Return"
- **Concierge Onboarding:** The RM handles all internal signature updates and mandate changes.
- **The "First Transaction" Milestone:** Guide the client through their first "Return Trade" (e.g., a single FX spot or payroll run) to ensure systems are live.

### Phase 4: Implementation of "Rule of 7"
In corporate banking, it takes 5-7 touchpoints to re-engage a cold lead.
1.  **Day 1:** Insight-heavy phone call/voicemail.
2.  **Day 2:** Follow-up 1-pager on the "Reactivation Incentive."
3.  **Day 7:** LinkedIn engagement on the client's company news.
4.  **Day 14:** Second call with a "Market Event" hook (e.g., rate shift).

## Real-World Case Studies

### Case Study 1: The "Stakeholder Reset" Success
- **Situation:** A $100M revenue manufacturer went dormant after the long-standing CFO retired. The account had $0 activity for 18 months.
- **Action:** The new RM used Scenario D (New Stakeholder) and shared a "Strategic Gap Analysis" showing the firm was paying 15% more in FX fees at their secondary bank.
- **Outcome:** Client reactivated the **Main Operating Account**.
- **Result:** $120k in annual revenue recovered + captured a $15M equipment financing mandate.

### Case Study 2: The "Service Recovery" Pivot
- **Situation:** A tech firm left the bank mentally because a "Trade Finance" request took 2 weeks to approve in 2023.
- **Action:** The RM called with an "Honest Reset" (Scenario E), demonstrating the bank's new **Digital Approval Workflow** (which reduced turnaround to 24 hours).
- **Outcome:** Secured a 3-month "Pilot Program" for their SE Asia imports.
- **Result:** Relationship RAROC improved from 0% (dormant) to 18%.

### Case Study 3: McKinsey-Style Portfolio Hygiene
- **Example:** A European bank implemented a "Radical Simplification" playbook in 2024.
- **Intervention:** They exited 15% of disengaged, low-margin accounts.
- **Result:** Free up **€2B in regulatory capital**, which was redeployed to high-growth sustainability sectors, yielding a 250bps improvement in group ROE.

## Industry Best Practices & Benchmarks
- **Reactivation Conversion:** Target **15-20%** of all disengaged accounts for annual reactivation.
- **The "90-Day Warning":** If an account has <3 logins in 90 days, it is **80% likely** to become fully dormant within 6 months.
- **KYC-to-Revenue Ratio:** High-performing portfolios maintain a ratio of **<$1 spent on KYC for every $10 in fee revenue.**
- **Retention Impact:** Clients who use **3+ sticky digital products** (e.g., API + Cash Sweeps) have a <1% dormancy rate.
- **Speed to Lead:** Following up on a "Silent Churn" signal within **5 business days** increases reactivation odds by 3x.

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **Dormancy Alert Dashboards:** Real-time CRM flags for "Negative Inactivity Anomalies."
- **Propensity to Reactivate (PtR) Models:** ML models that rank disengaged clients by their likelihood of returning based on past behaviors.
- **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Calculators:** To show dormant clients the "Hidden Cost" of their fragmented setup.
- **Digital KYC Portals:** (e.g., *Encompass* or *Fenergo*) to reduce the friction of "returning" to the bank.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **Reputational Risk:** An aggressive "Exit Strategy" can lead to negative PR. Always offer a **"Long Runway"** (90-120 days) for non-strategic clients to find a new bank.
- **Escheatment Compliance:** RMs must coordinate with Legal to ensure dormant funds are handled according to **Abandoned Property Laws** (e.g., Escheatment to the state after 3-5 years).
- **Anti-Money Laundering (AML):** Reactivating a long-dormant account requires a **"Full KYC Refresh."** RMs must not bypass compliance checks just to speed up the revenue "fix."
