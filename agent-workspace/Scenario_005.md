# Scenario 005: Credit Renewal Proactive Engagement

## Executive Summary
Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) view a credit renewal not as an administrative "check-the-box" task, but as a strategic **"Moment of Truth"** for the relationship. By initiating engagement 6–9 months before maturity, the RM shifts from a reactive posture to a proactive "Advisory" stance. This proactive window allows the RM to: 1) Manage "Renewal Shock" by smoothing interest rate transitions, 2) "Right-size" facility structures and covenants to match the client's current growth phase, 3) Block competitors (particularly private credit funds) through contractual "hooks" and "blend-and-extend" pricing, and 4) Use the renewal as a "wedge" to cross-sell capital-light ancillary products like FX and Transaction Banking. Success is measured by the **Retention Rate** (benchmark: 75–80%) and the **Incremental ROE** achieved through up-selling during the renewal cycle.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** This scenario is triggered when a credit facility is within **180 days (6 months)** of maturity, or when market data (e.g., a "Maturity Wall" surge in 2025) suggests a heightened risk of competitive displacement.
- **Stakeholders:** RM (Strategy), Credit Officer (Risk approval), Product Specialists (FX/Treasury for cross-sell), and the Client’s CFO or Treasurer.
- **Challenge:** "Renewal Shock." With nearly **$957 billion** in corporate debt set to mature in 2025, many clients face sudden, significant increases in debt-servicing costs. The RM must help the client navigate this transition while defending the bank’s interest margin.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply a "RAROC-driven" mental model to every renewal.

### Initial Assessment: The "Relationship Posture"
Before the first client call, the RM and Credit Officer define the bank's stance:
1. **Grow:** Client is high-value and under-leveraged. *Action: Propose increased limits and 3-5 year extension.*
2. **Hold:** Client is stable but profitability is marginal. *Action: Renew at market rates; demand "Ancillary Flow" to meet hurdle.*
3. **Exit/De-risk:** Client’s risk profile has deteriorated (PD has increased). *Action: Use renewal to tighten covenants, increase collateral (lower LGD), or facilitate a strategic exit.*

### Mental Models: The "Renewal Windows"
- **The "Safety Window" (T-180 to T-120):** Internal strategy and risk scan.
- **The "Education Window" (T-120 to T-90):** Client discovery and "Value Reporting" (showing the bank’s support).
- **The "Negotiation Window" (T-90 to T-30):** Closing the deal before the client "shops the market."

### Decision Logic: "Blend and Extend"
If market rates have risen, the RM may propose a **"Blend and Extend"** strategy. This combines the current (lower) rate with a new (higher) market rate over an extended term. This results in a "blended" rate that is higher than the original but lower than the current spot market rate, smoothing the client's transition.

## Expert Actions & Execution
Top RMs follow a 5-step "Proactive Renewal" pipeline:

### 1. Data-Driven Pre-Engagement (T-180)
- **Calculate the "Break-even Rate":** Determine the rate at which the bank is economically indifferent between the old and new loan.
- **Scan for EWIs:** Review "Early Warning Indicators" like covenant near-misses or declining liquidity. Address these *before* the credit committee meeting.

### 2. The "Renewal Readiness" Meeting (T-120)
- **The "Value Report":** Present a summary of how the bank enabled the client's success over the last 3 years (e.g., "We provided $50M in liquidity during your Q3 acquisition").
- **Discovery Questions:** Ask about their 3-year CapEx plan and "Dry Powder" needs for acquisitions. Move the conversation from "cost" to "growth."

### 3. Structural Optimization
- **Covenant Modernization:** Offer to "trade" a slight pricing increase for more flexible covenants (e.g., "Covenant-Lite" or "Springing Covenants") that give the CFO more operational headroom.
- **RWA Optimization:** Right-size consistently underutilized lines to reduce **Exposure at Default (EAD)** and free up the bank's capital.

### 4. The "Cross-Sell Wedge"
- **FX Hedging Trigger:** If the renewed facility funds international expansion, lead with **Interest Rate Swaps** or **Forward Rate Locks** to mitigate "Renewal Shock."
- **Transaction Hub Trigger:** Use the renewal as leverage to secure "Primary Operating Bank" status. Explain that the overall relationship profitability (RAROC) allows for a more competitive loan rate.

### 5. Defensive "Blocking" Tactics
- **Right of First Refusal (ROFR):** Ensure the credit agreement includes a ROFR, requiring the client to let the bank match any third-party offer before they can switch to a private credit fund.
- **Pre-emptive Extension:** Sign the renewal **60+ days early** to "take the client off the market" before competitors can pitch.

## Real-World Examples & Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Regional Bank - Cognitive Automation
- **Situation:** Rapid growth led to manual renewal bottlenecks and weeks of processing time.
- **Action:** Implemented machine learning to pull financial data and perform automated underwriting.
- **Outcome:** Reduced renewal processing time from weeks to **4–5 hours** and automated **75%** of manual tasks.

### Case Study 2: Major U.S. Bank - "Value-Based" Retention
- **Situation:** Bank of America used its "Life Plan" and "Balance Assist" tools to proactively engage clients during rate hikes.
- **Outcome:** Extended **1 million** proactive liquidity loans to help clients avoid "financial shock," driving record-high satisfaction and "sticky" relationships.

### Case Study 3: SME Digital Renewal Engine
- **Situation:** A private sector bank struggled with fragmented manual testing for SME renewals.
- **Action:** Deployed an automated loan origination framework.
- **Outcome:** Accelerated time-to-market by **40%** and enabled the processing of **5,000+ loans monthly**.

## Industry Best Practices
- **The "180-Day Rule":** Never start a renewal conversation less than 6 months out. Silence from a bank is often interpreted by CFOs as a pending denial, causing them to "shop."
- **RAROC Target:** Aim for an ROE of **15–18%** on the total relationship. If the loan alone is <10%, it must be supported by "Ancillary Flow" (FX/Cash).
- **Fixed-Rate Preference (2025):** Benchmark data shows **65%** of corporate borrowers are selecting fixed-rate renewals in 2025 (avg ~5.8%) to gain predictability.
- **Blocking Private Credit:** Use **Negative Pledges** and **Anti-Layering clauses** to block "unitranche" or "super-senior" structures used by direct lenders.

## Client Value Proposition
- **Liquidity Certainty:** "By renewing 90 days early, we eliminate your refinancing risk and ensure your $50M expansion project remains fully funded through any year-end market volatility."
- **Strategic Headroom:** "We’ve restructured your 'Leverage Ratio' covenant to give you an extra 0.5x cushion, allowing you to execute on that acquisition target we discussed."

- **AI-Driven Alerts:** Automated triggers that flag renewals at the T-180, T-120, and T-90 marks.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **Concentration Risk:** Use renewals to review industry exposure limits. If the sector is "over-weight," use the renewal to reduce the limit.
- **Refinance Risk:** If the bank cannot renew (e.g., due to credit deterioration), the RM has a **legal duty** to give the client sufficient time (180+ days) to find an alternative lender to avoid a liquidity crisis.
- **RWA Leakage:** Ensure all collateral valuations are updated during renewal to maximize regulatory capital relief.
