# Scenario 016: Leveraging Client Migration for Product Expansion

## Executive Summary
In the high-stakes environment of corporate banking, the "moment of victory"—winning a new client mandate—is actually the start of the most critical risk window in the relationship lifecycle. Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) recognize that the **"First 90 Days"** post-migration is a "Golden Window" for relationship entrenchment and **Product Expansion.** While the initial "Anchor" product (often a credit facility or a specific cash management mandate) gets the bank through the door, it rarely captures the client's full economic potential. Research indicates that a primary bank captures approximately **80% of a mid-market client's wallet**, but this status is won or lost during the implementation phase. An expert RM moves beyond "onboarding" to act as a **"Strategic Orchestrator,"** using the intensive data exchange of the setup process to identify and secure "quick-win" ancillaries like FX, Trade Finance, and Integrated Receivables.

The strategic mission in this scenario is to reduce the **"Time-to-Revenue"**—the interval from the first handshake to the first booked transaction across multiple product lines. By mining the "Intelligence Dossier" provided during KYC and technical ERP mapping, the RM uncovers "Unstated Needs" that the client may have forgotten to mention during the competitive RFP phase. For example, discovering a cluster of unmanaged international subsidiaries during a UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) check is an immediate trigger for a **Global Liquidity Pooling** pitch. Furthermore, by managing a seamless "Warm Handover" from the acquisition sales team to the coverage team, the RM ensures that the "Deal Rationale" (the *why* behind the switch) is translated into a 3-year **Digital Maturity Roadmap.**

Successful execution of this scenario transforms a "New-to-Bank" client into a "Digitally Entrenched" partner. By delivering operational excellence in the first 30 days and transitioning to high-value advisory by day 90, the RM builds the "Credit Credibility" and "Service Trust" needed to displace the client’s remaining secondary banks. Ultimately, this scenario is about **"Relationship Fertilization"**—ensuring the first seed of a loan grows into a multi-product forest of recurring fee income and low-cost deposits.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** 
    - **Systemic:** Client status in the **Customer Master Directory** transitions from "Prospect" to "Active."
    - **Operational:** Initiation of the "Implementation Kick-off" meeting for the anchor product.
    - **Data-Driven:** The RM reviews the **Intelligence Dossier** (KYC/AML files) and identifies external debt or cross-border flows not handled by the bank.
- **Stakeholders:** 
    - **Internal:** Acquisition Sales Executive (Hunter), Relationship Manager (Farmer/Orchestrator), Implementation Manager, Product Specialists.
    - **External:** CFO (Decision Maker), Treasurer (Technical Lead), CTO (ERP Integration).
- **The Challenge:** "Silo Loss." Information gathered during the 6-month sales cycle is often lost during the 1-week hand-off to the RM.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply the **"Onboarding-to-Advisory"** mental model.

### 1. The "Golden Window" Economics
The RM understands the "Retention Multiplier":
- **Single Product Client:** 70% chance of churn within 24 months.
- **3+ Product Client (within 90 days):** <5% churn risk.
- **Action:** Move the client to the "Rule of 3" before the first quarterly review.

### 2. KYC-to-Opportunity Mapping
The RM treats compliance data as **Sales Intelligence**:
- **Signal (KYC):** "High Interest Expense" on external financials. **Action:** Pitch Credit Consolidation/Refinancing.
- **Signal (KYC):** "Subsidiaries in Singapore/UAE." **Action:** Pitch FX Hedging and Local Clearing.
- **Signal (KYC):** "Founders nearing retirement age." **Action:** Referral to Private Banking/Wealth Management.

### 3. The "Service-to-Sales" Logic
The RM uses the "Technical Pain" of setup as a pitch point:
- *"I noticed your team is spending 10 hours a week mapping our bank statements to your SAP system. If we move your receivables to our **Integrated API Hub**, we can automate that entire process for you today."*

## Expert Actions & Execution: The 90-Day Sprint

### Phase 1: Days 1–30 | Seamless Onboarding & The "Listening Tour"
1.  **The Internal "Baton Pass":** The RM holds a 1-on-1 briefing with the Sales Executive to capture the "Jobs-to-be-Done" (JTBD) and "Discomfort Points" with the previous bank.
2.  **The "Success Plan" Presentation:** The RM meets the client and presents a **90-Day Vision.** This signals that the RM "owns" the client's long-term success, not just the initial loan.

### Phase 2: Days 31–60 | Operational Excellence & "Quick Wins"
1.  **Technical "Hyper-Care":** The RM oversees the "Penny Test" for new cash management portals, ensuring the client's first transaction is flawless.
2.  **Identifying the "Second Product":** Based on early transaction velocity (from **Detailed Ledger**), the RM pitches a low-friction ancillary (e.g., Purchasing Cards or automated Positive Pay) to solve an immediate operational pain point.

### Phase 3: Days 61–90 | Strategic Alignment & Wallet Capture
1.  **The "Post-Onboarding" Review:** Formal meeting to "Bless" the implementation and introduce **Product Specialists** (FX/DCM) for Phase 2 growth.
2.  **Top-to-Top Meeting:** Facilitate a call between the Bank's Regional Head and the client’s CEO. This cements the "Strategic Partner" status.

## Real-World Case Studies

### Case Study 1: The "SVB Influx" Expansion (JPMorgan Case)
- **Situation:** JPMorgan onboarded thousands of startups in a single weekend following a competitor's collapse.
- **Action:** Instead of just opening checking accounts, the bank deployed AI-driven "Onboarding Portals" that automatically suggested specialized treasury tools for venture-backed firms.
- **Outcome:** **Startup Banking revenue doubled** in 12 months.
- **Result:** Transitioned "Rescue Accounts" into "Full-Ecosystem" relationships using a 90-day expansion sprint.

### Case Study 2: Siemens Treasury Automation
- **Client:** A global industrial giant.
- **Action:** The lead bank used the implementation of a new blockchain-based payment rail to "Upsell" a comprehensive **Global Liquidity Forecasting** tool.
- **Outcome:** Replaced 3 secondary banks by proving the value of a "Single Source of Truth."
- **Result:** Captured **90% of the client's global cash volume** within the first 6 months.

### Case Study 3: The "KYC Lead" Success
- **Situation:** An RM noticed a client's KYC file listed a new factory opening in Mexico.
- **Action:** Before the client even mentioned it, the RM introduced the **Trade Finance** team to discuss "Maquiladora" financing and local payroll.
- **Outcome:** Won the $10M expansion mandate before the incumbent local bank could pitch.

## Industry Best Practices & Benchmarks
- **Time-to-Revenue Target:** Reduce from 100 days to **<45 days** through "Zero-Touch" digital onboarding.
- **The "Rule of 3":** Aim for **3 products per client** within the first 90 days to ensure a 95%+ retention rate.
- **Cross-Sell Velocity:** Expert RMs achieve a **30% increase in wallet share** within the first 180 days of a relationship.
- **Onboarding Satisfaction:** High-performing banks target a **Digital NPS of >50** specifically for the migration experience.
- **Lead-to-Mandate Ratio:** Convert **15-20% of KYC-identified leads** into active mandates within 6 months.

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **Intelligence Capture Dossiers:** Internal templates for Sales-to-RM hand-offs.
- **Next-Product-to-Buy (NPTB) Engines:** AI tools that scan new account profiles for "best fit" ancillaries.
- **Implementation Dashboards:** (e.g., *nCino* or *Finastra*) for real-time tracking of onboarding milestones.
- **Social Selling Platforms:** (e.g., *LinkedIn Sales Navigator*) to monitor C-suite hires at newly won clients.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **"Onboarding Fatigue" Risk:** Do not pitch too many products during a complex technical setup; prioritize **Quick Wins** that reduce client effort.
- **Incentive "Clawbacks":** Ensure Sales Executives are incentivized for **Retention** and **Deepening**, not just "Wins," to prevent low-quality migration.
- **Compliance Continuity:** Ensure that the "Relationship Narrative" in the **Account Plan** is updated immediately after onboarding to prevent KYC lapses in Year 1.
