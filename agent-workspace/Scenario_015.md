# Scenario 015: Incremental Revenue Targeting from Existing Products

## Executive Summary
In the competitive landscape of corporate banking, the most profitable growth is rarely found in new client acquisition; it is hidden within the "Internal Portfolio Gap" of existing relationships. This scenario focuses on the Relationship Manager’s (RM) ability to identify and capture **Incremental Revenue** from clients who are already using multiple bank products but at sub-optimal or "Secondary" levels. Expert RMs recognize that while the initial credit facility acts as the "Anchor," the real economic value of the relationship is unlocked through **Relationship Primacy**—the status of being the client’s Primary Financial Institution (PFI). By moving a client from a "Credit-only" or "Secondary" status to "Operating Primacy," an RM can capture 2x–3x more revenue without increasing the bank’s risk-weighted assets (RWA).

The strategic mission in this scenario is to conduct a surgical **"Wallet & Gap Analysis"** that moves beyond what the bank *is* doing to what the client *is doing elsewhere.* For a typical mid-market client, a primary bank captures approximately **63% of total financial products**, whereas a secondary bank is often relegated to a low-margin "price-taker" role, capturing less than 15%. An expert RM bridges this gap by quantifying the "Cost of Fragmentation" for the client—demonstrating how multi-banking leads to visibility gaps, reconciliation errors, and inefficient cash buffers. By pitching a "Consolidated Value Prop" that leverages volume-based pricing and integrated API data flows, the RM secures high-margin "flow" revenue (FX, Cash Management, Trade) that stabilizes the relationship and drives a significantly higher **Return on Equity (ROE).**

Successful execution requires the RM to navigate the "Multi-banking Defense"—the common treasurer objection of "not putting all eggs in one basket." By reframing the conversation from "credit risk diversification" to **"operational risk mitigation,"** the RM positions the bank as a strategic partner that understanding the client's business cycles better than any secondary provider. Ultimately, this scenario is about "Portfolio Mining"—using AI-driven triggers and peer benchmarking to ask for the next dollar of business before a competitor even realizes it is available.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** 
    - **Quantitative:** Discovery of a "Low SOW/High Wallet" profile in the **Monthly Profitability Aggregate** (e.g., $50M wallet, $5M captured).
    - **Behavioral:** A spike in outbound transfers to a competitor’s Routing Number (RTN) or a client inquiring about a product they currently use at a rival.
    - **Strategic:** The client's business growth (from **KPI Details**) has outpaced their usage of the bank’s transaction services.
- **Stakeholders:** 
    - **Internal:** RM (Orchestrator), Product Specialists (FX, Cash, Trade), Data Analyst (Wallet Sizing).
    - **External:** CFO (Decision Maker), Treasurer (Daily Ops), Controller.
- **The Challenge:** "Relationship Inertia." Clients often feel that their current "good enough" setup isn't worth the hassle of consolidation.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs apply the **"Wallet Allocation & Primacy"** mental model.

### 1. The Wallet Allocation Rule (WAR)
The RM uses the "Rank-Based" formula to estimate the gap without seeing competitor data:
- **WAR Score:** $\left( 1 - \frac{1}{\text{Rank} + 1} \right) \times \left( \frac{2}{\text{Number of Banks Used}} \right)$
- **Example:** If the bank is Rank 2 of 4, the WAR Score predicts a 33% SOW. If the actual capture is 15%, the RM has an **"Execution Gap"** of 18%.
- **Action:** Pitch a "Wedge Product" (e.g., a specific FX corridor) to move to Rank 1.

### 2. The "Cross-Sell Multiplier" Logic
The RM understands the correlation between product count and retention:
- **1 Product:** 20% churn risk.
- **3 Products:** 10% churn risk.
- **5+ Products:** <1% churn risk (**"The Strategic Lock-in"**).
- **Target:** Aim for at least 5 distinct service lines per client.

### 3. Decision Logic: "Underfed" vs. "Over-earned"
- **The "Underfed" Relationship:** High trust, high dialogue, but low revenue. **Action:** Be aggressive in asking for the operating business.
- **The "Over-earned" Relationship:** Revenue is high, but the relationship score is low. **Action:** Defensive play; fix service issues before asking for more wallet.

## Expert Actions & Execution: The Capture Pipeline

### Phase 1: The "Internal Wallet Audit" (Discovery)
The agent aggregates data to size the "White Space":
1.  **Peer Benchmarking:** *"90% of our Manufacturing clients use our automated receivables tool to reduce DSO. You are one of the 10% who doesn't. Shall we model the potential savings?"*
2.  **Proxy Sizing:** Use the client’s COGS (from KPI details) to estimate their **Trade Finance Wallet** (typically 1-2% of COGS).

### Phase 2: The "Operational Risk" Pivot
The RM counters the "Risk Diversification" objection by highlighting the **"Risk of Fragmentation"**:
- **Talk Track:** *"I understand the need for multiple credit providers. However, spreading your global payments across four portals creates 'Blind Spots' that AI-driven fraud can exploit. Let’s centralize your operations for visibility, while keeping your secondary banks for credit headroom."*

### Phase 3: The "Consolidated Value" Pitch
Quantify the **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**:
- **The "Power of One":** Show that by moving to the next "Volume Tier" in Cash Management, the client saves 25% on transaction fees across all subsidiaries.
- **Efficiency ROI:** *"By consolidating your FX flow, we can integrate the reporting directly into your SAP system, saving your team 15 hours of manual data entry every month."*

### Phase 4: The "Right to First Look" Mandate
- **The Renewal Lever:** During the next credit renewal, include a "Relationship Commitment" clause.
- **Action:** Negotiate a "Right of First Look" on all FX and M&A advisory business as a condition for "Tier 1" credit pricing.

| Benefit | Legacy (Secondary Bank) | Primacy (Primary Bank) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pricing** | Spot-rate / "Retail" | Volume-tiered / "Wholesale" |
| **Data Flow** | Manual / Fragmented | Real-time / Integrated (API) |
| **Advisory** | Product-led | Strategy-led (Sector Expert) |
| **Service** | General Helpdesk | Dedicated Relationship Team |

## Real-World Case Studies

### Case Study 1: The "FX Primacy" Pivot
- **Client:** A $250M revenue electronics importer.
- **Situation:** Bank provided a $20M Term Loan but only captured $2M in FX volume. The client used a boutique FX house for the rest.
- **Action:** The RM demonstrated that by consolidating FX flow, the client would qualify for the bank’s **"Market-Plus" Pricing Tier**, saving them $120k annually.
- **Outcome:** Captured **100% of the FX wallet**.
- **Result:** $150k in new annual fee income + 500% increase in relationship RAROC.

### Case Study 2: JPMorgan's "One Bank" Synergies
- **Example:** JPMorgan integrated its Commercial and Investment banks in 2024 to target mid-cap clients.
- **Impact:** Commercial clients generated over **$1B in IB fees** for the second consecutive year.
- **Result:** 44% surge in fees from existing clients simply by removing internal "silos" and offering advisory to lending clients.

### Case Study 3: The "Operating Primacy" Defensive Play
- **Situation:** A Tier 1 client was being poached by a competitor offering 10bps lower on a loan.
- **Action:** The RM showed that switching banks would cost the client's IT team **$200k in ERP re-mapping costs** for their integrated cash management.
- **Outcome:** Client stayed with the bank despite the higher loan rate.
- **Result:** Retained a $1M revenue relationship through "Digital Lock-in."

## Industry Best Practices & Benchmarks
- **The "Rule of 5":** Relationships with **5+ products** are nearly impossible to displace.
- **SOW Conversion Rate:** Top-quartile RMs convert **25% of identified "Wallet Gaps"** into active revenue within 12 months.
- **Fee-to-Interest Target:** Expert RMs aim for a **>0.5x ratio** (Fees = 50% of Interest).
- **Average Products per Client:** Institutional leaders (like HSBC or JPM) average **4.2 products** per corporate client.
- **Retention Impact:** Clients with integrated digital products have a **99% retention rate** over a 3-year period.

## Tools, Systems & Resources
- **Next Product to Buy (NPTB) Models:** Internal AI dashboards that flag "High Propensity" leads.
- **Wallet Sizing Workbenches:** Tools that use industry benchmarks to estimate a client's "External spend."
- **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Calculators:** Used during pitches to show the cost of multi-bank fragmentation.
- **Sector Multiples Reports:** To keep valuation and "Strategic Needs" top-of-mind for the client.

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **Concentration Risk:** If a client moves 100% of their business to you, ensure the bank is comfortable with the **Operational Reliance** they now have on your systems.
- **Pricing Cannibalization:** Ensure that a "Volume Discount" doesn't drop the product margin below the bank's hurdle rate.
- **Regulatory SOW Caps:** In some jurisdictions, single-bank exposure (credit + liquidity) is capped by law to prevent systemic risk.
