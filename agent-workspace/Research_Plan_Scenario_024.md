# Research Plan: Scenario 024 - Concentration Risk in Single Sector

## Scenario Summary
In corporate banking, "Sector Expertise" is a double-edged sword. While deep industry focus allows RMs to win more business, it can lead to dangerous **Portfolio Concentration.** Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) view their portfolio not just as a collection of clients, but as a "Risk Ecosystem." This scenario focuses on the identification and mitigation of single-sector concentration—where a downturn in a specific industry (e.g., Real Estate, Oil & Gas, or Retail) could jeopardize the bank’s entire regional book. By utilizing "Sector Triage" dashboards and cross-referencing internal exposure with macro-economic volatility signals, the RM develops a "Rebalancing Strategy" that involves selective de-risking of marginal clients and proactive prospecting in under-represented, low-correlation sectors.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for managing and mitigating single-sector concentration risk.
Secondary: Identify specific "Concentration Thresholds," sector correlation models, and best practices for "Portfolio Diversification" without losing sector-specific competitive edges.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "Optimal Concentration" Benchmark:** At what % of total portfolio exposure does a single sector become a "Concentration Risk" for a regional bank?
2. **Identifying High-Correlation Sectors:** How do expert RMs use "Cross-Sector Correlation" data (e.g., Hospitality vs. Aviation) to identify hidden risks?
3. **The "Rebalancing" Playbook:** What are the steps for a "Soft Exit" from a concentrated sector without damaging the bank's reputation?
4. **Sector-Specific Stress Testing:** How do RMs translate macro events (e.g., +200bps rate hike) into specific portfolio-level P&L impact for a concentrated book?
5. **The "Expertise vs. Risk" Trade-off:** How do top-tier banks maintain their "Specialist" status while strictly enforcing diversification limits?
6. **Data Signals for Success:** What external signals (Commodity prices, Regulatory shifts, Industry-specific delinquency rates) are the most reliable early warnings for a sector downturn?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for "Portfolio Resilience" (e.g., Diversification Index, Sector RAROC, Risk-Adjusted Revenue per Sector)?
