# Research Plan: Scenario 1 - High-Wallet Low-Capture Cross-Sell Targeting

## Scenario Summary
An RM identifies a client with a significant gross wallet size ($50M+) but the bank captures a low share (e.g., 15%). The RM must identify specific product buckets (trade, FX, cash) where the client spends externally, verify no risk constraints exist, and generate a prioritized action list with revenue potential.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Document how expert RMs identify, analyze, and execute cross-sell opportunities for under-penetrated high-value clients.
Secondary: Identify mental models for "wallet share" analysis, industry benchmarks for product-specific capture rates, and best practices for cross-sell pitching.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **Mental Models:** How do expert RMs distinguish between "addressable wallet" and "total wallet"? What frameworks guide the decision to pursue a client?
2. **Decision Logic:** What specific criteria (profitability, risk-adjusted returns, relationship depth) do top performers use to prioritize product pitches?
3. **Data & Benchmarks:** What are industry-standard capture rates (Share of Wallet - SOW) for different product lines (Cash Management, Trade Finance, FX) in corporate banking?
4. **Calculations:** What formulas do RMs use to estimate "Revenue at Risk" or "Revenue Potential" from under-penetrated wallets?
5. **Best Practices:** How do top-tier RMs (e.g., at JPMorgan, HSBC, Goldman Sachs) position cross-sell as a value-add rather than a transaction?
6. **Risk Management:** How do RMs verify credit capacity and risk appetite before proposing new business to a high-wallet client?
7. **Case Studies:** Real-world examples of successful "wallet capture" strategies and their outcomes (e.g., % increase in SOW, ROE impact).
