# Scenario 003: Deposit Growth Campaign Targeting

## Executive Summary
Expert Relationship Managers (RMs) approach deposit growth not as a "rate-chasing" exercise, but as a quest for **Operational Primacy**. In a post-Basel III regulatory environment, the value of a deposit is dictated by its "stickiness" and its impact on the bank’s **Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)**. Expert RMs prioritize **Operational Deposits** (25% run-off rate) over **Non-Operational** "hot money" (40-100% run-off rate) because operational cash requires less high-quality liquid asset (HQLA) backing, making it significantly more profitable for the bank. The strategic goal of this scenario is to move a client’s "operating account"—the hub of their payroll, payables, and receivables—to the bank, thereby securing low-cost funding and creating a high-barrier-to-exit relationship.

## Scenario Context
- **Triggers:** This scenario is triggered when a client has a high **Liquidity-to-Loan** ratio but low deposit balances at the bank, or when a "Liquidity Event" (M&A, IPO, Divestiture) is detected in the market.
- **Stakeholders:** RM (Lead), Treasury Management Officer (TMO), Deposit Desk/Liquidity Specialist, and the Client’s Treasurer or CFO.
- **Challenge:** The "Yield Gap." In a high-rate environment, corporate treasurers are under pressure to move excess cash into Money Market Funds (MMFs). The RM must combat this by highlighting the operational efficiency, tax benefits (via ECR), and safety (FDIC via ICS) of bank deposits.

## RM Thought Process & Decision Logic
Expert RMs categorize every dollar into a "Hierarchy of Value."

### Initial Assessment: The "Deposit Segmentation"
1. **Operational Cash (The "Sticky" Hub):** Funds used for daily operations (payroll, tax, vendor payments). These are the most valuable to the bank.
2. **Core Reserve (The "Safety" Buffer):** 3–6 months of operating expenses held for unforeseen needs.
3. **Surge/Excess Cash (The "Volatile" Yield-Seekers):** Cash from a recent capital raise or asset sale that is likely to leave for higher yield unless "trapped" via specialized products.

### Mental Models: The "Three S" Framework for Treasurers
When pitching for deposits, experts know Treasurers prioritize in this order:
1. **Safety:** Is my principal protected? (Counterparty risk).
2. **Stability/Liquidity:** Can I get my money when I need it? (Operational access).
3. **Spread/Yield:** What is my return? (Pricing).

### Decision Logic: ECR vs. Hard Interest
- **Lead with ECR (Earnings Credit Rate):** For clients with high service fees, ECR is a "soft credit" that is often more tax-efficient than hard interest because it is a "reduction in expense" rather than taxable income.
- **Lead with Yield:** Only for non-operational excess cash where the bank needs the liquidity for LCR/NSFR compliance and is willing to pay "Market Beta."

## Expert Actions & Execution
Top RMs follow a 5-step "Deposit Acquisition" playbook:

### 1. Identify "Held-Away" Balances
- **Transaction Mining:** Analyze outgoing wires in the "Monthly Profitability Aggregate." Frequent transfers to investment firms (Vanguard, BlackRock) or other banks signal "wallet leakage."
- **Event Monitoring:** Monitor S-1 filings, M&A announcements, and "Good Faith Deposit" signals in escrow transactions.

### 2. The "Operating Account" Pitch
- **ERP/TMS Integration:** Pitch **API-led connectivity** that links the bank directly to the client's SAP/Oracle system. Once "piped in," the account becomes operationally indispensable.
- **Virtual Account Management (VAM):** Offer VAM to allow the treasurer to create sub-ledgers for different projects or entities under one master account, simplifying their reconciliation.

### 3. Pricing Optimization (ECR & Bundling)
- **Calculate NVOD (Net Value of Deposits):** 
  - Formula: `NVOD = [(FTP Rate x (1 - Reserve)) - Interest Paid - Operational Costs] x Avg Balance`
  - Use high NVOD to justify waiving lending fees or tightening FX spreads.
- **ECR Strategy:** In 2024, set ECR at **70-90% of the 90-day T-Bill rate** to remain competitive without paying hard interest.

### 4. Capture "Surge" Balances via Specialized Products
- **Insured Cash Sweeps (ICS):** Use ICS/CDARS to provide FDIC insurance on multi-million dollar deposits by spreading them across a network of banks. This removes the "Safety" objection.
- **Escrow Services:** Position the bank as the **Escrow Agent** for M&A deals. Escrow funds are "sticky" by legal design and often stay for 12–24 months.

### 5. Defensive "Win-Back" Tactics
- **Alert on "Balance Decay":** If a client’s average daily balance drops by >20% in a month, trigger an immediate "Reason-to-Call" to discuss yield enhancement before they move to an external MMF.

## Real-World Examples & Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Large Bank "Deposit Desk" Implementation
- **Situation:** A bank used AI to monitor real-time inflows and outflows to identify "at-risk" deposits.
- **Action:** Created a specialized "Deposit Command Center" to provide RMs with daily alerts on large outflows.
- **Outcome:** SME deposits grew by **12%** and Corporate deposits by **7%** within 6 months by intercepting "flighty" cash with targeted retention offers.

### Case Study 2: Regional Bank - Digital Escrow Success
- **Situation:** A Northeastern bank targeted Law Firms and Property Managers for deposit growth.
- **Action:** Deployed a digital escrow platform that allowed clients to open sub-accounts in minutes instead of days.
- **Outcome:** The bank saw a **574% growth in deposits** within those specific verticals over 2 years.

### Case Study 3: Top-10 U.S. Bank - Predictive Growth
- **Situation:** Used predictive modeling to target households/businesses with >$100k in "held-away" investable assets.
- **Outcome:** Generated **$3 Billion** in new deposit balances within 6 months.

## Industry Best Practices
- **Segment-Specific Stickiness:** Focus on "High Stickiness" sectors: **Public Admin, Education, Faith-Based, and Non-Profits**. These sectors have low rate sensitivity and high operational needs.
- **The "25% Rule":** Always strive to classify corporate deposits as "Operational" under Basel III to minimize HQLA costs. If it's not operational, it's a "capital tax."
- **Deposit Beta Management:** Target a **Deposit Beta of <50%** for operational accounts. If the Beta hits 80-90%, the bank is "buying" the money, not "earning" it.
- **Relationship Bundling:** Clients using **3+ treasury services** (FX, Trade, Payroll) alongside deposits are 20% more profitable and significantly less likely to "rate-shop."

## Client Value Proposition
- **Operational Alpha:** "By consolidating your accounts into our VAM structure, you reduce your administrative workload by **30%** and gain real-time visibility across all entities."
- **Tax-Efficient Yield:** "Our ECR of 1.25% offsets your service fees dollar-for-dollar, which is effectively a **~1.60% pre-tax equivalent yield**."

## Risk Management & Considerations
- **Concentration Risk:** Avoid over-reliance on a few large corporate depositors. The "SVB lesson" proved that concentrated, non-operational deposits can flee at **20-30% per day**.
- **Interest Rate Risk:** Excessive high-rate deposits can "squeeze" the Net Interest Margin (NIM) if the bank cannot deploy the funds into high-yielding assets quickly.
- **Compliance:** Ensure adherence to **KYC/AML** for escrow and sub-accounting structures, particularly for "Commingled" accounts.
