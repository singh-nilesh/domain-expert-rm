# Research Plan: Scenario 010 - Subsidiary and Group Cross-Sell Coordination

## Scenario Summary
In multinational corporate banking, the "Parent-Subsidiary Gap" is a multi-million dollar opportunity. Often, a bank has a "Primary" relationship with the global Parent but is "Under-represented" at the subsidiary level, where local banks often hold the operating business. Expert RMs use a "Global Network Orchestration" model to identify these inconsistencies. By demonstrating the "Consolidation Benefit"—including group-wide liquidity visibility, fee rationalization, and unified risk reporting—the RM displaces local competitors and secures the entire group's wallet.

## Research Objectives
Primary: Map the expert RM's framework for coordinating cross-sell opportunities across a global corporate group structure.
Secondary: Identify specific "Consolidation Benefit" models, "Subsidiary-to-Parent" reporting gaps, and "Global Network" value propositions used by top-tier international banks.

## 7 Key Research Questions
1. **The "Global Coverage" Model:** How do expert Lead RMs (at the Parent level) coordinate with Subsidiary RMs to ensure a "One Bank" experience?
2. **Inconsistent Product Usage Signals:** What specific data points (group-wide) indicate that a subsidiary is using a competitor (e.g., local FX flow vs. parent-level hedging)?
3. **The "Consolidation Benefit" Calculation:** How do RMs quantify the value of moving from "Local Banks" to a "Global Network" (e.g., lower WACC, centralized liquidity)?
4. **Global Liquidity Optimization:** What are the mechanics of "Global Cash Pooling" or "Interest Aggregation" that drive subsidiary buy-in?
5. **Managing Internal Friction:** How do Lead RMs handle "Subsidiary Autonomy" and the local RM's relationship with the subsidiary CFO?
6. **The "Unified Group Pitch":** What are the components of a proposal that appeals to both the Global Treasurer and the Local Finance Director?
7. **Success Metrics:** What are the KPIs for "Group-Wide Penetration" (e.g., % of subsidiaries banked, Group SOW, Global Revenue per Client)?
